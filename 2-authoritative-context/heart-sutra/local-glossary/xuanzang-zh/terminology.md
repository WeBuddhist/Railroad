### amala (without stain)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 不垢 | 1 | characteristics of emptiness |

**Note:** Renders *amalā* as 不垢 ("not defiled"). Facheng renders the same term as 無垢 ("without stain") — closer to the Sanskrit etymology. Both render *vimalā* differently: Xuanzang 不淨, Facheng 離垢.

---

### vimala (without absence of stain)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 不淨 | 1 | characteristics of emptiness — "not pure" |

**Note:** *vimalā* in Sanskrit means "free from stain" (a positive state). Xuanzang's 不淨 ("not pure") renders this as a negation of purity rather than freedom from stain — a different framing. Facheng's 離垢 ("separated from stain") is closer to the Sanskrit.

---

### anūna (not deficient) / asaṁpūrṇa (not complete)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 不增不減 | 1 | characteristics of emptiness — "not increasing, not decreasing" |

**Note:** Sanskrit *anūnā asaṁpūrṇāḥ* means "not deficient, not complete" — ontological terms. Xuanzang renders as 不增不減 ("not increasing, not decreasing") — quantitative terms. Same divergence in Facheng (無減無增). Neither Chinese version follows the Sanskrit here. ⚑

---

### avidyā (ignorance)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 無明 | 2 | dependent origination negations |

**Note:** Xuanzang negates only *avidyā* (無無明). Both Sanskrit recensions also negate *vidyā* (knowledge). The *vidyā* negation is absent from Xuanzang and Facheng both. ⚑

### prāpti (attainment)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 得 | 2 | negation and bodhisattva section |

**Note:** Xuanzang has 無得 only. The longer Sanskrit also negates *aprāpti* (non-attainment) with *nāprāptiḥ*. Xuanzang omits this; Facheng preserves it as 無不得. ⚑

### cittāvaraṇa (mental veil/obstruction)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 罣礙 | 2 | bodhisattva section — "entanglement/impediment" |

**Note:** 罣礙 conveys the sense of being caught or hindered. Facheng renders *cittāvaraṇa* as 障礙 ("obstruction/hindrance"), which is more literal. Both are valid; 障礙 tracks the Sanskrit more closely.

### anuttarā samyaksaṁbodhi (unsurpassed complete awakening)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 | 1 | phonetic transliteration throughout |

**Note:** Xuanzang retains the phonetic transliteration rather than translating semantically. Facheng translates semantically as 無上正等菩提 ("unsurpassed, correct, and equal awakening"). Facheng's rendering is more transparent to readers without Sanskrit.

---

### mantra

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 咒 | 5 | standard term for mantra throughout |
| 大神咒 | 1 | first epithet — "great divine/spirit mantra" |
| 大明咒 | 1 | second epithet — *mahāvidyāmantro* |
| 無上咒 | 1 | third epithet — *anuttaramantro* |
| 無等等咒 | 1 | fourth epithet — *asamamantro* |

**Note:** 大神咒 adds 神 ("divine/spirit") with no Sanskrit basis — neither *prajñāpāramitāmantro* nor *mahāmantro* implies divine status. Facheng renders the equivalent as 大密咒 ("great secret mantra"), a different interpretive addition. ⚑

---

### gate gate pāragate pārasaṁgate bodhi svāhā (mantra)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 揭諦揭諦波羅揭諦波羅僧揭諦菩提薩婆訶 | 1 | phonetic transliteration |

**Note:** Xuanzang's phonetic convention: 揭諦 for *gate*, 波羅 for *pāra*, 波羅僧 for *pārasaṁ*, 菩提 for *bodhi*, 薩婆訶 for *svāhā*. Facheng uses: 峩帝 for *gate*, 波囉峩帝 for *pāragate* — a different phonetic system reflecting a later manuscript tradition.
