# Reference Glossary — Facheng 法成 (9th century CE)

*Extracted from translation. Key Sanskrit source terms with Facheng's Chinese renderings.
Where Facheng's rendering differs from Xuanzang's, the difference is noted.*

---

### prajñāpāramitā (般若波羅蜜多)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 般若波羅蜜多 | 5 | standard throughout |

**De facto standard:** 般若波羅蜜多

**Note:** Same semi-transliteration as Xuanzang — retained from the established Chinese Buddhist lexicon.

---

### skandha (aggregate)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 蘊 | 3 | 五蘊 and 蘊體性 |

**De facto standard:** 蘊

**Note:** Facheng uses 體性 to render *svabhāva* in the phrase "five aggregates empty of intrinsic nature" (五蘊體性皆空), giving 體性 for *svabhāva* — more explicit than Xuanzang's bare 皆空.

---

### svabhāva (intrinsic nature / own-being)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 體性 | 2 | five aggregates section — 五蘊體性悉皆是空, 五蘊體性皆空 |

**De facto standard:** 體性

**Note:** Xuanzang has no equivalent — his text says 五蘊皆空 without rendering *svabhāva* explicitly. Facheng's 體性 captures the Sanskrit more precisely.

---

### śūnyatā (emptiness)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 空 | 6 | standard throughout |
| 空性 | 3 | characteristics of emptiness section — 一切法空性, 空性之中 |

**De facto standard:** 空

**Note:** Facheng uses 空性 (emptiness-nature) where the Sanskrit uses *śūnyatā* as a grammatical subject with the sense of "the state of emptiness." This is a more precise rendering than bare 空 in these contexts. Xuanzang uses only 空 throughout.

---

### amala (without stain)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 無垢 | 1 | characteristics of emptiness |

**De facto standard:** 無垢

**Note:** Facheng 無垢 ("without stain") maps *amalā* more closely than Xuanzang's 不垢 ("not defiled"). The Sanskrit *a-mala* literally means "without (a-) stain (mala)" — Facheng's 無垢 captures this etymology precisely.

---

### vimala (free from stain)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 離垢 | 1 | characteristics of emptiness — "separated from / free of stain" |

**De facto standard:** 離垢

**Note:** Facheng 離垢 ("separated from stain / free from stain") renders *vimalā* more accurately than Xuanzang's 不淨 ("not pure"). *Vi-mala* in Sanskrit means "free from (vi-) stain (mala)" — a positive state, not a negation of purity. Facheng's rendering preserves this. ⚑ compared to Xuanzang.

---

### anūna (not deficient) / asaṁpūrṇa (not complete)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 無減無增 | 1 | characteristics of emptiness — "no decrease, no increase" |

**De facto standard:** 無減無增

**Note:** Sanskrit *anūnā asaṁpūrṇāḥ* = "not deficient, not complete" (ontological terms). Facheng 無減無增 = "no decrease, no increase" (quantitative terms). Same divergence as Xuanzang (不增不減 — same content, reversed order). Neither Chinese translation follows the Sanskrit here. ⚑

---

### bhagavān (Blessed One)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 薄伽梵 | 3 | narrative frame and closing — phonetic transliteration |
| 世尊 | 2 | narrative and closing — semantic: "World-Honoured One" |

**De facto standard:** 薄伽梵 (transliteration) / 世尊 (semantic) — used interchangeably

**Note:** Facheng uses both forms in the same text. 薄伽梵 is the transliteration of *bhagavān*; 世尊 is the standard Chinese semantic equivalent. Absent from Xuanzang (who has no narrative frame).

|---|---|---|

**De facto standard:** 具壽

**Note:** 具壽 is the standard Chinese rendering of *āyuṣmān* ("possessing life/long-lived" — an honorific for elder monks). Absent from Xuanzang.

### buddhānubhāva (Buddha's power/majesty)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|

**De facto standard:** 佛威力

**Note:** *Anubhāva* = power, majesty, influence. Facheng's 威力 ("awesome power/might") captures this well. Absent from Xuanzang (no dialogue frame).

### prāpti / aprāpti (attainment / non-attainment)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 無得亦無不得 | 1 | negation series — "no attainment and also no non-attainment" |

**De facto standard:** 無得亦無不得

**Note:** Facheng preserves both *na prāptir* (無得) AND *nāprāptiḥ* (無不得), matching the longer Sanskrit *na prāptir nāprāptiḥ* exactly. Xuanzang has only 無得 — the negation of non-attainment is absent from Xuanzang. This is one of the clearest alignments between Facheng and the longer Sanskrit recension. ⚑

---

### aprāptitva (non-attainment — causal)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 無所得 | 1 | bodhisattva section — 以無所得故 "because of non-attainment" |

**De facto standard:** 以無所得故

**Note:** Corresponds to *aprāptitvena* in the longer Sanskrit. Absent from the shorter Sanskrit. Both Facheng and Xuanzang have this phrase, confirming both Chinese translations follow the longer recension at this point.

### cittāvaraṇa (mental obstruction/veil)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 障礙 | 2 | bodhisattva section — 心無障礙 "mind without obstruction" |

**De facto standard:** 障礙

**Note:** Facheng 障礙 ("obstruction/hindrance") tracks *āvaraṇa* (veil, obstruction) more literally than Xuanzang's 罣礙 ("entanglement"). *Citta-āvaraṇa* = mental obstruction; 心障礙 = mental obstruction. Facheng's rendering is more precise.

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 顛倒 | 1 | bodhisattva section — 超過顛倒 "transcended inversion" |

**De facto standard:** 顛倒

**Note:** Facheng uses 超過 ("transcended/gone beyond") for *atikrānta* rather than Xuanzang's 遠離 ("far separated from"). 超過 maps *ati-krānta* more directly. Same concept, slightly different emphasis.

### anuttarā samyaksaṁbodhi (unsurpassed complete awakening)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 無上正等菩提 | 1 | buddhas of three times section |

**De facto standard:** 無上正等菩提

**Note:** Facheng translates semantically — 無上 = *anuttarā* ("unsurpassed"), 正等 = *samyak* ("correct and equal"), 菩提 = *bodhi* ("awakening"). Xuanzang phonetically transliterates as 阿耨多羅三藐三菩提. Facheng's rendering is more transparent to readers without Sanskrit.

---

### mantra

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 咒 | 6 | standard term for mantra |
| 大密咒 | 1 | first epithet — "great secret mantra" |
| 大明咒 | 1 | second epithet = *mahāvidyāmantro* |
| 無上咒 | 1 | third epithet = *anuttaramantro* |
| 無等等咒 | 1 | fourth epithet = *asamamantro* |
| 祕密咒 | 1 | closing summary — "secret mantra" |

**De facto standard for mantra:** 咒

**Note:** Facheng renders the first epithet as 大密咒 ("great secret mantra"). Neither Sanskrit recension supports 密 ("secret"). Xuanzang uses 大神咒 ("great divine mantra") for the same epithet — a different interpretive addition. The Sanskrit *prajñāpāramitāmantro* implies no secrecy or divinity. Both Chinese translators add a qualifier absent from the source. ⚑

---

### gate gate pāragate pārasaṁgate bodhi svāhā (mantra)

| Rendering | Frequency | Contexts |
|---|---|---|
| 峩帝峩帝波囉峩帝波囉僧峩帝菩提莎訶 | 1 | phonetic transliteration |

**Note:** Facheng's phonetic convention differs from Xuanzang's: 峩帝 for *gate* (vs. Xuanzang 揭諦), 波囉 for *pāra* (vs. Xuanzang 波羅), 莎訶 for *svāhā* (vs. Xuanzang 薩婆訶). These differences reflect distinct phonetic transcription systems and likely different manuscript traditions, not different underlying mantras — the Sanskrit source is identical.
