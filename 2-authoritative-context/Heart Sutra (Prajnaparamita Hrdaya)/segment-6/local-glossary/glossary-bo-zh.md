## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་ vs ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་**: Some commentaries (C8, C10, C12) omit the genitive 'འི་' (zab mo'i) in 'ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་', reading 'ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་'. This is a grammatical variation that does not alter the meaning.
- ⚑ **ལ་ཡང་ vs ཀྱང་**: Some commentaries (C2, C12) use 'ཀྱང་' (kyang) instead of 'ལ་ཡང་' (la yang) for 'also on'. Both particles convey the same meaning.
- ⚑ **བལྟ་ vs རྟོགས་/ལྟོས་**: Commentary 12 uses 'རྟོགས་' (rtogs, to realize) instead of 'བལྟ་' (blta, to observe) for the second verb. Commentary 10 uses 'ལྟོས་' (ltos, to look at/relate to) for the first verb. The primary reading 'བལྟ་' (to observe) is maintained as it is supported by the majority of commentaries.

## Standardized text

No meaning-altering textual variants were found upon comparison with available commentaries. Minor grammatical and stylistic variations were noted (e.g., 'ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་' vs 'ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་', 'ལ་ཡང་' vs 'ཀྱང་'), but these do not alter the core semantic meaning of the text. The verb 'བལྟ་' (to observe) is consistently supported by the majority of commentaries, with some offering interpretive variations like 'རྟོགས་' (to realize) or 'ལྟོས་' (to look at/relate to) which are considered divergences rather than corrections.

## Term analysis

### `ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་ (zab mo'i spyod pa)`

Some commentaries (C8, C10, C12) present 'ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་' (zab mo spyod pa), omitting the genitive particle 'འི་'. This is a common grammatical or metrical variation in Tibetan texts and does not alter the semantic meaning of 'profound practice'. Therefore, it is not considered a meaning-altering discrepancy.

### `ཕུང་པོ་ལྔ་པོ་དེ་དག་ལ་ཡང་ (phung po lnga po de dag la yang)`

Some commentaries (C2, C12) use 'ཕུང་པོ་ལྔ་པོ་དེ་དག་ཀྱང་' (phung po lnga po de dag kyang) where 'ཀྱང་' (kyang) replaces 'ལ་ཡང་' (la yang). Both particles convey the meaning of 'also' or 'even on', and this variation does not fundamentally alter the semantic meaning of 'also on those five skandhas'. It is a stylistic or grammatical choice, not a meaning-altering variant.

### `རྣམ་པར་བལྟ་ཞིང་ (rnam par blta zhing) / རྣམ་པར་བལྟའོ། (rnam par blta'o)`

The verbs 'བལྟ་' (blta, to observe/perceive) are consistently used in the source text. While Commentary 12 uses 'རྟོགས་' (rtogs, to realize) for the second verb, and Commentary 10 uses 'ལྟོས་' (ltos, to look at/relate to) for the first verb, the majority of commentaries and the UCCA interpretation support 'བལྟ་'. The variations 'རྟོགས་' and 'ལྟོས་' are considered interpretive divergences that emphasize a particular aspect (realization or relation) rather than fundamental textual corrections of the core meaning 'to observe'. Therefore, no meaning-altering discrepancy is found for these terms.
