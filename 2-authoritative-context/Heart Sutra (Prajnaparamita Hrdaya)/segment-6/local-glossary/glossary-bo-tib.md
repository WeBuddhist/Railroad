## Term analysis

### `ཡང་དེའི་ཚེ་ (yang de'i tshe)`

The phrase 'ཡང་དེའི་ཚེ་' (yang de'i tshe, again at that time) is consistently interpreted across commentaries (e.g., Commentary 1, 3, 5, 12, 13). While Commentary 3 and 12 offer explanations of the particle 'ཡང་' (yang) as 'certainly' or 'emphasis', these are interpretive nuances of a particle and do not constitute a meaning-altering textual variant. The core temporal meaning remains unchanged.

### `བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའ་སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོ་འཕགས་པ་སྤྱན་རས་གཟིགས་དབང་ཕྱུག་ (byang chub sems dpa' sems dpa' chen po 'phags pa spyan ras gzigs dbang phyug)`

This compound refers to 'Bodhisattva Mahasattva Noble Avalokiteshvara'. All commentaries (e.g., Commentary 1, 3, 5, 8, 10, 12) agree on the identification and titles of the subject. Extensive explanations are provided for each component of the title, but no textual variants that alter the meaning of the subject were found.

### `ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་ཉིད་ལ་ (shes rab kyi pha rol tu phyin pa zab mo'i spyod pa nyid la)`

This phrase means 'on the very profound practice of the perfection of wisdom'. Some commentaries (e.g., Commentary 8, 10, 12) present 'ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་ཉིད་ལ་' (zab mo spyod pa nyid la) without the genitive particle 'འི་' ('i) between 'ཟབ་མོ་' (zab mo, profound) and 'སྤྱོད་པ་' (spyod pa, practice). This omission of a genitive particle is a common grammatical variation or metrical contraction in Tibetan, especially in verse or condensed prose, and does not alter the semantic meaning of 'profound practice'. Therefore, it is not considered a meaning-altering discrepancy.

### `རྣམ་པར་བལྟ་ཞིང་ (rnam par blta zhing)`

The phrase 'རྣམ་པར་བལྟ་ཞིང་' (rnam par blta zhing, perceiving thoroughly) is consistently interpreted as 'to look', 'to perceive', or 'to discern' across various commentaries (e.g., Commentary 3, 5, 6, 8). There are no textual variants that change its meaning.

### `ཕུང་པོ་ལྔ་པོ་དེ་དག་ལ་ཡང་ (phung po lnga po de dag la yang)`

This phrase means 'also on those five aggregates'. Some commentaries (e.g., Commentary 2, 12) use 'ཕུང་པོ་ལྔ་པོ་དེ་དག་ཀྱང་' (phung po lnga po de dag kyang) with 'ཀྱང་' (kyang) instead of 'ལ་ཡང་' (la yang). Both 'ཀྱང་' and 'ཡང་' function as particles meaning 'also' or 'even', and 'ལ་' (la) is a locative/dative marker. The substitution of 'ལ་ཡང་' with 'ཀྱང་' is a common stylistic or grammatical variation that does not alter the core meaning of 'also on those five aggregates'. Commentary 4 explicitly notes 'ཀྱང་གི་སྒྲས་' (by the word kyang) as an alternative. Therefore, it is not a meaning-altering discrepancy.

### `རང་བཞིན་གྱིས་སྟོང་པར་ (rang bzhin gyis stong par)`

The phrase 'རང་བཞིན་གྱིས་སྟོང་པར་' (rang bzhin gyis stong par, as empty of self-nature) is consistently presented and interpreted across all relevant commentaries (e.g., Commentary 2, 4, 5, 7, 9, 11, 12, 13). There are no textual variants that alter its meaning.

### `རྣམ་པར་བལྟའོ། (rnam par blta'o)`

The concluding verb 'རྣམ་པར་བལྟའོ།' (rnam par blta'o, perceives thoroughly) is consistent across all commentaries. No meaning-altering variants were found for this term.
