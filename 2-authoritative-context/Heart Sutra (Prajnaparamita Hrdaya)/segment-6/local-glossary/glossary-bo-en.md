## Term analysis

### `ཟབ་མོའི་ (zab mo'i) vs ཟབ་མོ་ (zab mo)`

The source text uses 'ཟབ་མོའི་' (zab mo'i, genitive case), making 'profound' an attribute of 'perfection of wisdom' (i.e., 'the practice of the profound perfection of wisdom'). Some commentaries (8, 10, 12) use 'ཟབ་མོ་' (zab mo, nominative/absolutive), making 'profound' an attribute directly of 'practice' (i.e., 'the profound practice of the perfection of wisdom'). This is a minor grammatical variation that does not fundamentally alter the semantic meaning of the phrase. The source text's reading is supported by UCCA and other commentaries (1, 5, 13) and is therefore retained as authoritative.
