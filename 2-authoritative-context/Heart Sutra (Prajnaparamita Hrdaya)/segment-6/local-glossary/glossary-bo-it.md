## Term analysis

### `ཡང་དེའི་ཚེ་ (yang de'i tshe)`

Commentaries 1, 3, 5, 8, 10, 12, and 13 explain the temporal adverb 'ཡང་དེའི་ཚེ་' (then, at that time) without presenting any meaning-altering textual variants. The interpretations focus on the context and function of 'ཡང་' and 'དེའི་ཚེ་'.

### `བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའ་སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོ་འཕགས་པ་སྤྱན་རས་གཟིགས་དབང་ཕྱུག་ (byang chub sems dpa' sems dpa' chen po 'phags pa spyan ras gzigs dbang phyug)`

This compound refers to the full title of Avalokiteshvara. Commentaries 1, 3, 5, 8, 10, 12, and 13 consistently use or explain these terms without any meaning-altering textual variants. The variations are in the explanatory expansions of each component of the title, not in the terms themselves.

### `ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་ཉིད་ལ་ (shes rab kyi pha rol tu phyin pa zab mo'i spyod pa nyid la)`

This phrase means 'on the very profound practice of the Perfection of Wisdom'. Commentaries 1, 3, 5, 6, 8, 10, 12, and 13 explain the individual components ('Perfection of Wisdom', 'profound', 'practice') and their meaning in context. Commentary 6 interprets 'ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་' as 'ཟབ་མོ་མངོན་སུམ་དུ་རྟོགས་ཤིང་སྤྱོད་པའི་ཡེ་ཤེས' (wisdom that realizes and practices the profound), which is an interpretive expansion rather than a textual variant. Commentary 13 interprets 'སྤྱོད་པ་' as 'ཆོས་ཀྱི་དབྱིངས་ཀྱི་རང་བཞིན་དེ་ཁོ་ན་ཉིད་' (the very nature of the dharmadhātu), which is an explanation of the object of practice, not a change to the source text's wording. No meaning-altering textual variants were found.

### `རྣམ་པར་བལྟ་ཞིང་ (rnam par blta zhing)`

This means 'thoroughly contemplating/observing'. Commentaries 3, 5, 6, and 12 explain or use this phrase. Commentary 5 interprets 'རྣམ་པར་བལྟ་' as 'རྣམ་པར་རྟོག་པ་' (conceptualizing/discriminating), which is an interpretation of the nature of the contemplation rather than a direct textual variant. The core meaning of 'contemplating' is preserved. No meaning-altering textual variants were found.

### `ཕུང་པོ་ལྔ་པོ་དེ་དག་ལ་ཡང་ (phung po lnga po de dag la yang)`

This means 'also on those five aggregates'. Commentaries 2, 4, 5, 7, 9, 11, 12, and 13 address this phrase. Commentaries 2 and 12 use 'དེ་དག་ཀྱང་' (de dag kyang) instead of 'དེ་དག་ལ་ཡང་'. Both 'ཀྱང་' and 'ཡང་' function as 'also/even', and the presence or absence of the locative particle 'ལ་' (la) is a common grammatical/stylistic variation that does not fundamentally alter the semantic meaning of 'also on those five aggregates'. Commentary 5 uses 'རྣམས་ལ་ཡང་' (rnams la yang) which is a pluralizer plus 'also on', semantically equivalent to 'དེ་དག་ལ་ཡང་'. No meaning-altering textual variants were found.

### `རང་བཞིན་གྱིས་སྟོང་པར་ (rang bzhin gyis stong par)`

This means 'as empty by nature'. Commentaries 2, 4, 5, 7, 9, 11, 12, and 13 explain this concept. Commentary 4 uses 'ངོ་བོ་ཉིད་ཀྱིས་སྟོང་པ་' (ngo bo nyid kyis stong pa), which is a direct synonym for 'རང་བཞིན་གྱིས་སྟོང་པ་' (rang bzhin gyis stong pa), both meaning 'empty by nature/essence'. While a different term, it conveys the identical philosophical meaning and therefore is not considered a meaning-altering variant. Commentary 7 elaborates with 'རང་བཞིན་གྱི་ངོ་བོས་མ་གྲུབ་ཅིང་སྟོངས་པར' (not established by nature and empty), which is an explanation rather than a variant. No meaning-altering textual variants were found.

### `རྣམ་པར་བལྟའོ། ། (rnam par blta'o)`

This is the concluding verb, meaning 'thoroughly contemplates/sees'. Commentaries explain its meaning in context. No meaning-altering textual variants were found.
