## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ vs འཕགས་པ་**: Commentary 1 uses 'འཕགས་པ་' (phags pa, Noble One) instead of 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (bcom ldan 'das ma, Blessed Lady). The primary reading 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' is adopted as it is supported by Commentaries 5 and 7, and is the standard epithet for Prajñāpāramitā.

## Standardized text

The source text 'བོད་སྐད་དུ། བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྙིང་པོ།' is the authoritative reading for the title. Commentary 1 introduces a variant by replacing 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (Blessed Lady) with 'འཕགས་པ་' (Noble One), which is a meaning-altering term substitution. However, this variant is not adopted as the source text's reading is confirmed by Commentaries 5 and 7, and is consistent with the widely accepted title of the text. No correction to the source text is required.

## Term analysis

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ (bcom ldan 'das ma)` (discrepancy)

Commentary 1 presents a meaning-altering variant, replacing 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (bcom ldan 'das ma, Blessed Lady) with 'འཕགས་པ་' (phags pa, Noble One). This is a direct term substitution that changes the epithet of the subject. However, the source text's reading 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' is supported by Commentaries 5 and 7, and aligns with the standard title of the Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya Sūtra. Therefore, the source text is considered authoritative for this term, and no correction is applied.
