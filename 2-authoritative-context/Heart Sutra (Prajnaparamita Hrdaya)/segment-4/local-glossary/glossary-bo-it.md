## Term analysis

### `འདི་སྐད་བདག་གིས་ཐོས་པ་དུས་གཅིག་ན།`

This phrase, 'Thus I have heard at one time,' is consistently interpreted across all relevant commentaries (Commentaries 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8, 10). No meaning-altering variants were identified. The discussions in commentaries focus on the identity of 'I' (བདག་གིས་) or the implications of 'at one time' (དུས་གཅིག་ན།), but the words themselves and their core meaning remain unchanged.

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་རྒྱལ་པོའི་ཁབ་བྱ་རྒོད་ཕུང་པོའི་རི་ལ་`

This phrase, referring to 'The Bhagavān at Rajagriha, on Vulture Peak mountain,' is consistently affirmed by all commentaries (Commentaries 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10). No meaning-altering variants were found for the names of the Bhagavān or the location.

### `དགེ་སློང་གི་དགེ་འདུན་ཆེན་པོ་དང་། བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའི་དགེ་འདུན་ཆེན་པོ་དང་ཐབས་ཅིག་ཏུ་བཞུགས་ཏེ།`

This phrase describes 'a great assembly of monks and a great assembly of bodhisattvas, together resided.' Commentaries (1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10) consistently support this meaning. The variation between 'དང་ཐབས་ཅིག་ཏུ་' (dang thabs cig tu) in the source text and 'དང་ཐབས་གཅིག་ཏུ་' (dang thabs gcig tu) in some commentaries (e.g., Commentary 1, 10) is a common orthographic or metrical variation of the same term meaning 'together with' and does not alter the semantic meaning. Therefore, no meaning-altering discrepancy was found.
