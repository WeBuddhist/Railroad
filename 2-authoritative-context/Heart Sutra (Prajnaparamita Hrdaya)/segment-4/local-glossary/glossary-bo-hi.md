## Term analysis

### `འདི་སྐད་བདག་གིས་ཐོས་པ་དུས་གཅིག་ན།`

The phrase 'འདི་སྐད་བདག་གིས་ཐོས་པ་དུས་གཅིག་ན།' (thus I have heard at one time) is consistently rendered across all consulted Tibetan commentaries (Commentaries 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8, 10) and perfectly aligns with the Sanskrit 'एवम् मया श्रुतम् एकस्मिन् समये' (Evam mayā śrutam ekasmin samaye). No meaning-altering variants or significant grammatical expansions were identified.

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་`

The term 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་' (bcom ldan 'das, Bhagavan) is consistently used in the source text and all commentaries (Commentaries 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10). It directly corresponds to the Sanskrit 'भगवान्' (Bhagavān). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `རྒྱལ་པོའི་ཁབ་བྱ་རྒོད་ཕུང་པོའི་རི་ལ་`

The location 'རྒྱལ་པོའི་ཁབ་བྱ་རྒོད་ཕུང་པོའི་རི་ལ་' (rgyal po'i khab bya rgod phung po'i ri la, at Vulture Peak Mountain in Rajagriha) is consistently present in the source text and all commentaries (Commentaries 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10). It aligns with the Sanskrit 'राजगृहे गृध्रकूटे पर्वते' (Rājagṛhe Gṛdhrakūṭe parvate). No meaning-altering variants were found.

The phrase 'དགེ་སློང་གི་དགེ་འདུན་ཆེན་པོ་དང་།' (dge slong gi dge 'dun chen po dang, with a great assembly of bhikshus) is consistent across the source text and all commentaries (Commentaries 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10).
