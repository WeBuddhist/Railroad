## Standardized text

No meaning-altering textual variants were found in the source text. All commentaries and the UCCA semantic interpretation align with the provided text. The Sanskrit 'Evaṃ mayā' (ཨེ་བཾ་མཱ་ཡ།) in Commentary 8 confirms the opening phrase 'འདི་སྐད་བདག་གིས་ཐོས་པ་'.

## Term analysis

### `འདི་སྐད་བདག་གིས་ཐོས་པ་དུས་གཅིག་ན།`

The phrase 'འདི་སྐད་བདག་གིས་ཐོས་པ་དུས་གཅིག་ན།' (thus have I heard at one time) is consistently present across all commentaries and is directly confirmed by the Sanskrit 'Evaṃ mayā' (ཨེ་བཾ་མཱ་ཡ།) in Commentary 8, which is the standard opening for sutras. No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་`

The term 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་' (Blessed One) is consistently used and explained across all commentaries without any textual variation that would alter its meaning. Commentaries elaborate on its meaning but do not present alternative readings.

### `རྒྱལ་པོའི་ཁབ་བྱ་རྒོད་ཕུང་པོའི་རི་ལ་`

The location 'རྒྱལ་པོའི་ཁབ་བྱ་རྒོད་ཕུང་པོའི་རི་ལ་' (in Rājagṛha at Vulture Peak mountain) is consistently cited and described in all commentaries. No meaning-altering variants were found; commentaries only provide geographical or symbolic explanations.

### `དགེ་སློང་གི་དགེ་འདུན་ཆེན་པོ་དང་།`

The phrase 'དགེ་སློང་གི་དགེ་འདུན་ཆེན་པོ་དང་།' (together with a great community of monks) is consistently present. Some commentaries expand on the qualities or number of the monks (e.g., 'ཉན་ཐོས་དགྲ་བཅོམ་པའི་དགེ་འདུན་གྱི་ཚོགས་ཆེན་པོ་'), but these are elaborations, not meaning-altering textual variants of the original phrase.

### `བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའི་དགེ་འདུན་ཆེན་པོ་དང་ཐབས་ཅིག་ཏུ་བཞུགས་ཏེ།`

The phrase 'བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའི་དགེ་འདུན་ཆེན་པོ་དང་ཐབས་ཅིག་ཏུ་བཞུགས་ཏེ།' (and a great community of bodhisattvas, was dwelling together) is consistently present. Commentaries elaborate on the qualities of the bodhisattvas or the nature of their dwelling, but no meaning-altering textual variants were found. The addition of 'སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོའི་' in some commentaries (e.g., Commentary 3 and 5) is a common prose expansion and does not alter the core meaning of 'bodhisattva'.
