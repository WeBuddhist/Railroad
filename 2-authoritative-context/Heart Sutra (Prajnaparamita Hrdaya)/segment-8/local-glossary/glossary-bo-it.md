## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་ vs རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་འམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་**: The source text uses `རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་` (son of noble family), while multiple commentaries (1, 2, 16, 18) expand this to `རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་འམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་` (son or daughter of noble family) to clarify the inclusive scope of the participant. The expanded reading is adopted in the standardized text.
- ⚑ **ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་**: Commentary 1 omits `སྤྱོད་པ་` (conduct), reading `ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱད་པར་` (to practice the profound [thing]). Commentary 18 omits `ཟབ་མོའི་` (profound's), reading `སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་` (to practice the conduct). Commentaries 12 and 16 read `ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་` (profound conduct), omitting the genitive `འི་`. Commentary 8 interprets `སྤྱོད་པ་` as the agent and `སྤྱད་པ་` as the object, which is a significant reinterpretation. The source text's reading `ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་` is retained as authoritative for its completeness and semantic clarity.

## Term analysis

### `རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་ (rig kyi bu)` (discrepancy)

The source text uses `རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་` (son of noble family). Multiple commentaries (1, 2, 16, 18) expand this to `རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་འམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་` (son or daughter of noble family). This expansion clarifies the intended inclusive scope of the participant, which is a semantic clarification, and is therefore considered a meaning-altering variant in terms of scope. No Sanskrit text was provided to override this common interpretive expansion.

### `ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་ (zab mo'i spyod pa spyad par)`

Commentary 1 omits `སྤྱོད་པ་` (conduct), reading `ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱད་པར་` (to practice the profound [thing]). Commentary 18 omits `ཟབ་མོའི་` (profound's), reading `སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་` (to practice the conduct). Commentaries 12 and 16 read `ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་` (profound conduct), omitting the genitive `འི་`. Commentary 8 interprets `སྤྱོད་པ་` as the agent and `སྤྱད་པ་` as the object, which is a significant reinterpretation. However, the source text's reading `ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་` (to practice the profound conduct of Prajnaparamita) is semantically complete and aligns with the UCCA analysis, and no Sanskrit is available to suggest an alternative. Therefore, the source text is retained as authoritative for this phrase, and these are noted as divergences rather than corrections.
