## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་**: Many commentaries (C1, C2, C10, C16, C18, C20) expand 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་' (rig kyi bu, son of noble family) to 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུའམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་' (rig kyi bu'am rigs kyi bu mo, son or daughter of noble family) to include both genders. This is an interpretive expansion, not a semantic change to the term itself.
- ⚑ **སྤྱོད་པ་**: Commentary 8 interprets 'སྤྱོད་པ་' (spyod pa) as 'གང་ཟག་སྟེ་བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའོ།' (gang zag ste byang chub sems dpa'o, the person, i.e., the Bodhisattva), whereas the primary reading (adopted above) and other commentaries interpret it as 'conduct' or 'practice'.
- ⚑ **སྨྲས་པ་**: Commentary 4 interprets 'སྨྲས་པ་' (smras pa, said/spoken, perfect tense) as 'སྨྲས་པར་འགྱུར་བ་' (smras par 'gyur ba, will be said, future tense). The primary reading (adopted above) maintains the perfect tense, which is consistent with the narrative flow of a question being posed.

## Standardized text

No meaning-altering corrections were applied to the text. While divergences were noted for 'སྤྱོད་པ་' (spyod pa) and 'སྨྲས་པ་' (smras pa), the source text's readings are considered authoritative based on contextual coherence and broader commentary agreement. For 'སྤྱོད་པ་', Commentary 8's interpretation as 'person/Bodhisattva' is a semantic shift from the standard 'conduct/practice'. For 'སྨྲས་པ་', Commentary 4's future tense interpretation ('will be said') is a tense change from the source's perfect tense ('said'), but the perfect tense is required by the narrative flow of a question being posed.

## Term analysis

### `རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་ (rig kyi bu)`

Several commentaries (C1, C2, C10, C16, C18, C20) expand 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་' (son of noble family) to 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུའམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་' (son or daughter of noble family). This is a common interpretive expansion to clarify inclusivity, not a meaning-altering variant of the original term itself. The core meaning of 'son of noble family' is preserved, and the instruction is simply extended in scope.

### `སྤྱོད་པ་ (spyod pa)` (discrepancy)

Commentary 8 interprets 'སྤྱོད་པ་' (spyod pa) as 'གང་ཟག་སྟེ་བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའོ།' (gang zag ste byang chub sems dpa'o), meaning 'the person, i.e., the Bodhisattva'. This fundamentally alters the meaning from 'profound conduct/practice' (as in 'ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་') to 'profound Bodhisattva'. The source text's reading of 'conduct' or 'practice' is consistent with the UCCA interpretation and other commentaries (e.g., C18, C2) and is considered authoritative in this context. This is a meaning-altering semantic variant.

### `སྤྱད་པར་འདོད་པ་ (spyad par 'dod pa)`

Commentary 5 mentions an alternative reading 'སྤྱད་པ་བྱེད་པར་འདོད་པ་' (spyad pa byed par 'dod pa) from 'some texts'. This is a minor grammatical expansion ('to wish to practice' vs. 'to wish to do the practice') that does not alter the core semantic meaning. It is not considered a meaning-altering variant.

### `སྨྲས་པ་ (smras pa)` (discrepancy)

Commentary 4 interprets 'སྨྲས་པ་' (smras pa, perfect tense 'said' or 'spoken') as 'སྨྲས་པར་འགྱུར་བ་' (smras par 'gyur ba, future tense 'will be said'). This constitutes a tense change, which is a meaning-altering variant. However, the narrative context of a question being posed (and subsequently answered) strongly supports the perfect tense 'said' or 'asked' as the authoritative reading. Other commentaries (e.g., C6, C11, C18) either match the source's tense or offer synonymous perfect tense verbs. Therefore, the source text's reading is retained.
