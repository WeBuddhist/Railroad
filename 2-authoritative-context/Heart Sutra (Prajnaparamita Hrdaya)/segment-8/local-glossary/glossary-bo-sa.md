## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་ vs རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་འམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་**: The source text reads 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་' (rig kyi bu, son of noble family), but the Sanskrit 'कुलपुत्रो वा कुलदुहिता वा' (kulaputro vā kuladuhitā vā) and multiple Tibetan commentaries (C1, C2, C10, C16, C18, C20) include 'or daughter of noble family' (རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་). The expanded form is adopted as primary to reflect the full scope of practitioners.

## Standardized text

རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་འམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་གང་ལ་ལ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་འདོད་པ་དེས་ཇི་ལྟར་བསླབ་པར་བྱ། དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་སྨྲས་པ་དང་།

A meaning-altering omission was corrected by expanding 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་' (son of noble family) to 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་འམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་' (son of noble family or daughter of noble family). This correction aligns with the Sanskrit 'कुलपुत्रो वा कुलदुहिता वा' (kulaputro vā kuladuhitā vā) and is supported by multiple Tibetan commentaries (C1, C2, C10, C16, C18, C20) that include both genders when discussing the practitioner. The metrical structure of the verse is not applicable here as this is a prose passage.

## Term analysis

### `རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་ (rig kyi bu)` (discrepancy)

The source text's 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་' (rig kyi bu, son of noble family) is an incomplete rendering of the practitioner. The Sanskrit 'कुलपुत्रो वा कुलदुहिता वा' (kulaputro vā kuladuhitā vā, son of noble family or daughter of noble family) explicitly includes both genders. Multiple Tibetan commentaries (C1, C2, C10, C16, C18, C20) also expand this phrase to include 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་' (rig kyi bu mo, daughter of noble family), indicating a common understanding that the shorter form is an omission that alters the scope of who is addressed. This is a meaning-altering variant.

### `ཇི་ལྟར་བསླབ་པར་བྱ། (ji ltar bslab par bya)`

This phrase 'how should one train?' accurately represents the question being asked by Shariputra. While the subsequent Sanskrit text provides Avalokiteshvara's answer starting with 'तेनैवं व्यवलोकितव्यम्' (tenaivaṃ vyavalokitavyam, thus should be seen/examined), this is part of the response, not a variant of the question itself. Therefore, no meaning-altering discrepancy is found for this term in the context of the question.

### `དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་སྨྲས་པ་དང་། (de skad ces smras pa dang)`

This phrase 'thus having said, and...' perfectly matches the meaning of the Sanskrit 'एवमुक्ते' (evamukte, thus having been said) which introduces Avalokiteshvara's response. No meaning-altering discrepancy is found.
