## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་ vs རིགས་ཀྱི་བུའམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་**: Several commentaries (e.g., C1, C2, C16, C18, C20) expand 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་' (rig kyi bu, son of noble family) to include 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་' (rig kyi bu mo, daughter of noble family), broadening the scope to 'son or daughter of noble family'. This is an expansion, not a semantic alteration.

## Term analysis

### `རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་ (rig kyi bu)`

Commentaries 1, 2, 16, 18, and 20 expand 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་' (rig kyi bu, son of noble family) to 'རིགས་ཀྱི་བུའམ་རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་མོ་' (rig kyi bu'am rig kyi bu mo, son or daughter of noble family). This is a common expansion of scope to include both genders, but it does not alter the fundamental meaning of 'noble one' and is not considered a meaning-altering textual variant.

### `སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་ (spyod pa spyad par)`

The phrase 'སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་' (spyod pa spyad par, to practice the practice) is consistent across most commentaries. Commentary 1 uses 'སྤྱད་པར་' (spyad par) directly, omitting the noun 'སྤྱོད་པ་'. Commentary 5 explicitly states that 'བྱེད་པ་' (byed pa, doing) and 'སྒྲུབ་པ་' (sgrub pa, accomplishing) are semantically equivalent to 'སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་', indicating that these variations do not alter the core meaning. Commentary 8 interprets 'སྤྱོད་པ་' as the person and 'སྤྱད་པ་' as the Dharma, which is a semantic interpretation rather than a textual variant. No meaning-altering discrepancy found.
