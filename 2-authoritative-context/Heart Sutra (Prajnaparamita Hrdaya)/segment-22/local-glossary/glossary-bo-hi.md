## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **ཏདྱ་ཐཱ་ (tadyathā)**: Commentary 6 uses 'ཏད་ཡཱ་ཐཱ་ (tad yā thā)'; Commentary 5 uses 'ཏ་དྱ་ཐཱ་ (ta dyā thā)'. These are transliteration variants.
- ⚑ **ག་ཏེ་ (gate)**: Commentary 1 uses 'གཏེག་ཏེ་ (gteg te)'; Commentary 6 uses 'གྷ་ཏེ་ (gha te)'. These are transliteration variants.
- ⚑ **པཱ་ར་ག་ཏེ་ (pāragate)**: Commentary 4 uses 'པ་ར་ག་ཏེ་ (pa ra ga te)'; Commentary 6 uses 'སྤ་ར་གྷ་ཏེ་ (spa ra gha te)'. These are transliteration variants. Commentary 3 interprets 'པ་ར་ (pa ra)' as 'supreme, reality' and 'ག་ཏེ་ (ga te)' as 'realized', breaking the compound.
- ⚑ **པཱ་ར་སཾ་ག་ཏེ་ (pārasaṃgate)**: Commentary 1 uses 'པཱ་ར་སམ་ག་ཏེ་ (pā ra sam ga te)'; Commentary 5 uses 'པཱ་ར་སངྒ་ཏེ་ (pā ra sang ga te)'; Commentary 6 uses 'སྤ་ར་སང་གྷ་ཏེ་ (spa ra sang gha te)'. These are transliteration variants.
- ⚑ **བོ་དྷི་ (bodhi)**: Commentary 1 uses 'བྷོ་དི་ (bho di)'; Commentary 6 uses 'བྷོ་དེ་ (bho de)'. These are transliteration variants. Commentary 4 interprets 'བོ་དྷི་ (bodhi)' as 'uninterrupted compassion', diverging from the standard 'enlightenment'.
- ⚑ **སྭ་ཧཱ་ (svāhā)**: Commentaries 1, 3, 5, 6, 7 use 'སྭཱཧཱ་ (svā hā)'. This is a common transliteration variant. Commentary 4 interprets 'སྭཱ་ཧཱ་ (svā hā)' as 'the tantras are naturally liberated', diverging from the standard 'may it be established'.

## Standardized text

No meaning-altering textual variants were found in the source text 'ཏདྱ་ཐཱ། ག་ཏེ་ག་ཏེ་པཱ་ར་ག་ཏེ། པཱ་ར་སཾ་ག་ཏེ། བོ་དྷི་སྭ་ཧཱ།'. Minor spelling and transliteration differences were noted across commentaries (e.g., ཏདྱ་ཐཱ་ vs ཏད་ཡཱ་ཐཱ་, ག་ཏེ་ vs གཏེག་ཏེ་/གྷ་ཏེ་, པཱ་ར་ vs པ་ར་/སྤ་ར་, སཾ་ vs སམ་/སང་, བོ་དྷི་ vs བྷོ་དི་/བྷོ་དེ་, སྭ་ཧཱ་ vs སྭཱཧཱ་), but these do not alter the semantic meaning. While Commentary 4 offered divergent interpretations for 'བོ་དྷི་' (bodhi) and 'སྭ་ཧཱ་' (svāhā), these were not supported by the UCCA or the majority of other commentaries and are considered interpretive outliers rather than textual variants requiring standardization.

## Term analysis

### `ཏདྱ་ཐཱ་`

The term 'ཏདྱ་ཐཱ་' (tadyathā) shows minor spelling variations across commentaries (e.g., 'ཏད་ཡཱ་ཐཱ་' in Commentary 6, 'ཏ་དྱ་ཐཱ་' in Commentary 5), but these are transliteration differences and do not alter the meaning 'thus' or 'it is like this', which is consistently supported by all commentaries and the UCCA interpretation.

### `ག་ཏེ་`

The term 'ག་ཏེ་' (gate) appears twice. Commentaries show minor spelling variations such as 'གཏེག་ཏེ་' (Commentary 1) and 'གྷ་ཏེ་' (Commentary 6), but these are transliteration differences. The core meaning 'gone' or 'go' is consistently maintained across all commentaries and the UCCA interpretation, indicating no meaning-altering discrepancy.

### `པཱ་ར་ག་ཏེ་`

The term 'པཱ་ར་ག་ཏེ་' (pāragate) shows minor spelling variations like 'པ་ར་ག་ཏེ་' (Commentary 4) and 'སྤ་ར་གྷ་ཏེ་' (Commentary 6). While Commentary 3 breaks it into 'པ་ར་' (supreme) and 'ག་ཏེ་' (realized), and Commentary 4 offers a nuanced interpretation, the overarching meaning of 'gone to the other shore' or 'gone beyond' is consistent with the UCCA and the majority of commentaries. These are not meaning-altering variants.

### `པཱ་ར་སཾ་ག་ཏེ་`

The term 'པཱ་ར་སཾ་ག་ཏེ་' (pārasaṃgate) exhibits minor spelling variations such as 'པཱ་ར་སམ་ག་ཏེ་' (Commentary 1) and 'པཱ་ར་སངྒ་ཏེ་' (Commentary 5). Similar to 'པཱ་ར་ག་ཏེ་', some commentaries offer slightly different breakdowns, but the fundamental meaning of 'gone completely to the other shore' or 'gone perfectly beyond' is preserved across the UCCA and most commentaries. These are not meaning-altering variants.

### `བོ་དྷི་`

The term 'བོ་དྷི་' (bodhi) shows minor spelling variations like 'བྷོ་དི་' (Commentary 1) and 'བྷོ་དེ་' (Commentary 6). Commentary 4 interprets 'བོ་དྷི་' as 'uninterrupted compassion', which is a significant interpretive divergence from the standard meaning of 'enlightenment'. However, the UCCA and the majority of commentaries (1, 3, 5, 7) consistently interpret 'bodhi' as 'enlightenment'. Given the overwhelming consensus and the standard meaning of 'bodhi' in the Prajñāpāramitā context, Commentary 4's interpretation is considered an outlier and not a meaning-altering textual variant of the source text itself. Thus, no correction is warranted.

### `སྭ་ཧཱ་`

The term 'སྭ་ཧཱ་' (svāhā) shows minor spelling variations like 'སྭཱཧཱ་' (Commentary 1). Commentary 4 provides a highly divergent interpretation ('the tantras are naturally liberated'), but the majority of commentaries (1, 3, 5, 7) and the UCCA consistently interpret it as 'may it be established' or 'may it become'. Therefore, Commentary 4's interpretation is an outlier and not a meaning-altering textual variant of the source text. No correction is needed.
