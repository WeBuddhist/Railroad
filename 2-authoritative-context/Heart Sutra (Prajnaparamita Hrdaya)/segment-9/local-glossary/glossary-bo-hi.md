## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **སྤྱན་རས་གཟིགས་ (spyan ras gzigs) vs སྤྱན་རས་གཟིག་ (spyan ras gzig)**: Commentary 20 uses 'སྤྱན་རས་གཟིག་' (spyan ras gzig), which is a common spelling variation or typo for 'གཟིགས་' (gzigs) in the context of Avalokiteshvara. The source text's 'གཟིགས་' is retained as authoritative.
- ⚑ **ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་ (zab mo'i spyod pa) vs ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱད་པ་ (zab mo spyad pa)**: Commentaries 10 and 16 read 'ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱད་པ་' (zab mo spyad pa). While grammatically different, the meaning of 'profound conduct/practice' is preserved. The source text's 'ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་' is retained as authoritative.
- ⚑ **རྣམ་པར་ (rnam par) vs རྣམ་པ་ (rnam pa)**: Commentary 18 uses 'རྣམ་པ་' (rnam pa, noun 'aspect/form') instead of the adverbial 'རྣམ་པར་' (rnam par, 'thoroughly/in various ways'). This is a meaning-altering variant, but the source text's 'རྣམ་པར་' is supported by other commentaries and UCCA, and is retained as authoritative.
- ⚑ **དེ་དག་ཀྱང་ (de dag kyang) vs དེ་དག་ལ་ཡང་ (de dag la yang)**: Commentary 2 uses 'དེ་དག་ལ་ཡང་' (de dag la yang). Both are inclusive particles and do not alter the core meaning. The source text's 'དེ་དག་ཀྱང་' is retained.
- ⚑ **རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ། (rnam par yang dag par rjes su blta'o) vs ཡང་དག་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ། (yang dag par rjes su blta'o)**: Commentaries 2 and 5 omit 'རྣམ་པར་' (rnam par). This is a stylistic difference, and the source text's more complete reading is retained.
- ⚑ **རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ། (rnam par yang dag par rjes su blta'o) vs རྣམ་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ། (rnam par rjes su blta'o)**: Commentary 14 omits 'ཡང་དག་པར་' (yang dag par, 'correctly'). ## Term analysis

### `སྤྱན་རས་གཟིགས་ (spyan ras gzigs)`

Commentary 20 uses 'སྤྱན་རས་གཟིག་' (spyan ras gzig). This is a common spelling variation or typo for 'གཟིགས་' (gzigs) in the context of Avalokiteshvara. While 'གཟིག་' can mean 'leopard', here it is clearly intended as 'gzigs' (honorific 'to see'). It does not alter the identity of the Bodhisattva or the core meaning of the text, thus not a meaning-altering variant.

### `ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་ (zab mo'i spyod pa)`

Commentaries 10 and 16 read 'ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱད་པ་' (zab mo spyad pa) instead of the source's 'ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་' (zab mo'i spyod pa). This represents a subtle grammatical difference in how 'profound practice/conduct' is constructed (genitive vs. direct compound or nominalized verb). However, the overall meaning of 'profound conduct/practice' is preserved, and the source text's reading is grammatically sound and common. Therefore, it is not considered a meaning-altering variant.

### `རྣམ་པར་ (rnam par) [in རྣམ་པར་བལྟ་བར་བྱ་སྟེ།]`

Commentary 18 has 'རྣམ་པ་བལྟ་བར་བྱ་སྟེ་' (rnam pa blta bar bya ste), using the noun 'རྣམ་པ་' (rnam pa, aspect/form) instead of the adverbial 'རྣམ་པར་' (rnam par, thoroughly/in various ways) found in the source text. This is a meaning-altering variant, as it changes the instruction from 'look thoroughly' to 'look at the aspect'. However, the source text's reading with 'རྣམ་པར་' is supported by UCCA and other commentaries (e.g., Commentary 4's explanation of 'རྣམ་པར་བལྟ་བར་བྱ་སྟེ་' as 'རྣམ་པ་སྣ་ཚོགས་སུ་སོ་སོར་བརྟག་པར་བྱའོ།' - 'to examine individually in various aspects'). Therefore, the source text is authoritative and no correction is needed.

### `དེ་དག་ཀྱང་ (de dag kyang)`

Commentary 2 uses 'དེ་དག་ལ་ཡང་' (de dag la yang) instead of 'དེ་དག་ཀྱང་' (de dag kyang). Both 'ཀྱང་' (kyang) and 'ལ་ཡང་' (la yang) function as inclusive particles ('also', 'even'). This is a stylistic variation that does not alter the core meaning. The source text's reading is retained.

### `རྣམ་པར་ (rnam par) [in རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ།]`

Commentaries 2 and 5 omit 'རྣམ་པར་' (rnam par) before 'ཡང་དག་པར་' (yang dag par), reading 'ཡང་དག་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ།'. While 'རྣམ་པར་' adds emphasis or thoroughness, its omission does not fundamentally alter the meaning of 'correctly seen in accordance'. This is considered a stylistic difference, and the source text's more complete reading is retained.

### `ཡང་དག་པར་ (yang dag par)`

Commentary 14 omits 'ཡང་དག་པར་' (yang dag par, correctly), reading 'རྣམ་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ།'. The omission of 'correctly' removes a significant nuance from the instruction to 'thoroughly see in accordance'. This is a semantic difference. However, the source text's reading, which includes 'ཡང་དག་པར་', is more precise and complete, and is therefore considered authoritative. No correction is needed.
