## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་ vs ཡང་དག་པར་**: Commentaries 2, 17, and 19 omit 'རྣམ་པར་' (rnam par, perfectly), while Commentary 14 omits 'ཡང་དག་པར་' (yang dag par, truly/correctly). The source text's full phrase 'རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་' (perfectly and truly) is retained as primary.
- ⚑ **རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་ vs རྣམ་པར་སོ་སོར་རྟོག་པའི་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཡང་དག་པར་**: Commentary 20 adds 'སོ་སོར་རྟོག་པའི་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་' (so sor rtog pa'i shes rab kyi, by the wisdom of individual analysis) as an interpretive expansion before 'ཡང་དག་པར་'.

## Term analysis

### `ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་`

The source text's 'ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་' (zab mo'i spyod pa spyad par, to practice the profound conduct) was compared with Commentary 10's 'ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱད་པ་སྤྱོད་པ་' (zab mo spyad pa spyod pa, to practice the profound practice). While there is a slight grammatical difference (genitive 'འི་' vs. direct juxtaposition), Commentary 10's explanation clarifies that 'spyad pa' is the object and 'spyod pa' is the manner of practice. The overall semantic meaning of 'wishing to practice the profound conduct/practice' remains consistent across both readings and is supported by parallel translations (e.g., VmWa: '希望修习甚深般若波罗蜜多的行持'). This is not a meaning-altering variant.

### `རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་`

The source text's 'རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་' (rnam par yang dag par, perfectly and truly) was compared with various commentary readings. Commentary 2, 17, and 19 omit 'རྣམ་པར་' (rnam par, perfectly), while Commentary 14 omits 'ཡང་དག་པར་' (yang dag par, truly/correctly). Commentary 20 adds 'སོ་སོར་རྟོག་པའི་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་' (so sor rtog pa'i shes rab kyi, by the wisdom of individual analysis) as an interpretive expansion. While these represent minor textual variations in emphasis or grammatical completeness, the core meaning of 'observing correctly/truly' is preserved in all readings. The full phrase 'perfectly and truly' in the source text is supported by the comprehensive nature of the observation implied in Prajnaparamita. Therefore, these are not considered meaning-altering discrepancies.
