## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་ vs སྤྱད་པ་སྤྱོད་པར་**: The source text reads ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་ (zab mo'i spyod pa spyad par), meaning 'to engage in the profound practice'. Commentary 10 presents a variant ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱད་པ་སྤྱོད་པར་ (zab mo spyad pa spyod par), interpreting སྤྱད་པ་ (spyad pa) as the 'object of practice' and སྤྱོད་པར་ (spyod par) as 'to practice'. The source text's reading is adopted as primary.
⚑ **རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་ vs ཡང་དག་པར་**: The source text includes རྣམ་པར་ (rnam par, thoroughly) in the phrase རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་ (rnam par yang dag par, thoroughly and correctly). Commentaries 2, 5, 14, 17, and 19 omit རྣམ་པར་, reading ཡང་དག་པར་ (yang dag par, correctly). The source text's more comprehensive reading is adopted as primary.

## Standardized text

Analysis identified two semantic divergences in the commentaries but no meaning-altering corrections were applied to the source text. The phrase ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་ (zab mo'i spyod pa spyad par) from the source text, meaning 'to engage in the profound practice', was retained as authoritative despite Commentary 10's alternative interpretation of སྤྱད་པ་སྤྱོད་པར་ (spyad pa spyod par) as 'to practice the object of practice'. Similarly, the source text's རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ། (rnam par yang dag par rjes su blta'o), meaning 'should thoroughly and correctly observe', was retained. While several commentaries omit རྣམ་པར་ (rnam par), the source's inclusion of 'thoroughly' is considered the more complete and authoritative reading, adding a nuance of scope to the observation. No metrical considerations were relevant as no changes were made.

## Term analysis

### `སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་` (discrepancy)

The source text reads ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་སྤྱད་པར་ (shes rab kyi pha rol tu phyin pa zab mo'i spyod pa spyad par), meaning 'to engage in the profound practice of the perfection of wisdom'. Commentary 10 presents a variant ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱད་པ་སྤྱོད་པར་ (zab mo spyad pa spyod par), interpreting སྤྱད་པ་ (spyad pa) as the 'object of practice' (i.e., Prajnaparamita itself) and སྤྱོད་པར་ (spyod par) as 'to practice'. This constitutes a semantic difference in the grammatical roles and meaning of the compound. However, the source text's reading is widely supported by other commentaries and is a standard phrasing in Prajnaparamita literature. No correction is made to the source text, which is considered authoritative.

### `རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་` (discrepancy)

The source text includes རྣམ་པར་ (rnam par), meaning 'thoroughly' or 'completely', as part of the adverbial phrase རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་ (rnam par yang dag par, thoroughly and correctly). Several commentaries (2, 5, 14, 17, 19) omit རྣམ་པར་, resulting in ཡང་དག་པར་ (yang dag par, correctly). This omission semantically reduces the scope or intensity of the observation from 'thoroughly and correctly' to merely 'correctly'. The source text's inclusion of རྣམ་པར་ is considered the more complete and authoritative reading, and thus no correction is applied.
