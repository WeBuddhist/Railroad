## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **ཡང་དག་པར་ (yang dag par)**: Commentary 14 omits 'ཡང་དག་པར་' (correctly/perfectly), suggesting a reading of 'should be seen thoroughly' rather than 'should be seen thoroughly and correctly'. The source text and most other commentaries include 'ཡང་དག་པར་', which is adopted as the primary reading.

## Term analysis

### `ཤཱ་ར་དྭ་ཏའི་བུ་ (shA ra dwa ta'i bu) vs ཤཱ་རིའི་བུ་ (shA ri'i bu)`

The source text uses the full patronymic 'ཤཱ་ར་དྭ་ཏའི་བུ་' (son of Śāradvatī) in the initial address, and the abbreviated 'ཤཱ་རིའི་བུ་' (son of Śāri) in the direct address. Both refer to Śāriputra. This is a common variation in naming conventions and does not alter the semantic meaning of the referent. Commentaries consistently use both forms.

### `ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་ (zab mo'i spyod pa) vs ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་ (zab mo spyod pa)`

The source text uses 'ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་' (zab mo'i spyod pa, lit. 'conduct of profundity' or 'profound conduct'). Commentary 18 uses 'ཟབ་མོ་སྤྱོད་པ་' (zab mo spyod pa, 'profound conduct'). This is a minor grammatical variation (genitive case marker 'འི་' vs. adjective form) that does not fundamentally alter the meaning of 'profound conduct'.

### `རྣམ་པར་ཡང་དག་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ། (rnam par yang dag par rjes su blta'o)`

Commentary 14 omits 'ཡང་དག་པར་' (yang dag par, correctly/perfectly), rendering it 'རྣམ་པར་རྗེས་སུ་བལྟའོ།' (rnam par rjes su blta'o, should be seen thoroughly). While the omission of 'ཡང་དག་པར་' does subtly change the nuance from 'should be seen perfectly/correctly' to 'should be seen thoroughly/completely', the source text's reading, including 'ཡང་དག་པར་', is supported by the majority of other commentaries (C2, C5, C8, C11, C17, C19, C20). Therefore, the source text is considered authoritative, and no correction is warranted. This is noted as a divergence in the 'divergences' field.
