## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་ vs དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་**: Commentaries 1 and 2 use 'དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་' (de skad ces), meaning 'saying that', while the source text and most other commentaries use 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་' (di skad ces), meaning 'saying this'. Both introduce direct speech, and the difference is not considered semantically significant enough to warrant a correction.

## Term analysis

### `འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་ (di skad ces)`

The source text uses 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་' (di skad ces, saying this), while Commentaries 1 and 2 use 'དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་' (de skad ces, saying that). Both phrases function to introduce direct speech. While 'འདི་' (this) typically refers to something immediately present or about to be presented, and 'དེ་' (that) refers to something already mentioned or more distant, in the context of introducing a subsequent dialogue, the semantic difference is not considered meaning-altering. The majority of commentaries (3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9) and the UCCA semantic interpretation align with the source text's 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་'. Therefore, no meaning-altering discrepancy is found, and no standardization is required.
