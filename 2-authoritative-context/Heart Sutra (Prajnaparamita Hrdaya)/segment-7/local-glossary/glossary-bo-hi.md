No meaning-altering textual variants were found in the source text. While Commentary 2 uses 'དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་' (de skad ces) instead of 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་' (di skad ces), this is a minor deictic difference ('that' vs 'this') that does not alter the core meaning of 'thus'. Furthermore, although the verb 'སྨྲས་སོ།' (smras so, said) is used, multiple commentaries (Commentary 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 9) consistently clarify that what was 'said' was a 'question' regarding the practice of Prajnaparamita. ## Term analysis

### `འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་ (di skad ces)`

Commentary 2 uses 'དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་' (de skad ces) instead of 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་' (di skad ces). This is a minor deictic difference ('that' vs 'this') that does not alter the core meaning of 'thus' or 'in this manner'. Therefore, it is not considered a meaning-altering textual variant.

### `སྨྲས་སོ། (smras so)`

The term 'སྨྲས་སོ།' (smras so) means 'said' or 'spoke'. While the literal translation is 'said', multiple commentaries (Commentary 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 9) consistently clarify that what was 'said' was a 'question' regarding the practice of Prajnaparamita. This is a contextual interpretation of the speech act rather than a textual variant of the word itself. The word 'སྨྲས་སོ།' is not substituted by 'དྲིས་སོ།' (dris so, asked) in any commentary's main text, but its function is explained. Thus, it is not a meaning-altering textual variant requiring correction of the source text.
