## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་ vs དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་**: Commentaries 2 and 6 read `དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་ (de skad ces)` ('that thus'), while the source text and Sanskrit `एतद्` (etad) support `འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་ (di skad ces)` ('this thus'). The latter is adopted as primary.

## Standardized text

དེ་ནས་སངས་རྒྱས་ཀྱི་མཐུས། ཚེ་དང་ལྡན་པ་ཤཱ་རིའི་བུས་བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོ་འཕགས་པ་སྤྱན་རས་གཟིགས་དབང་ཕྱུག་ལ་འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་སྨྲས་སོ། །

A meaning-altering variant was identified where Commentaries 2 and 6 read 'དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་' (de skad ces, 'that thus') instead of the source text's 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་' (di skad ces, 'this thus'). The Sanskrit text 'एतद्' (etad) in 'एतदवोचत्' confirms the source text's reading of 'this' as authoritative. Therefore, the original source text is retained as the standardized text. Citation: Sanskrit 'एतदवोचत्' and Commentaries 2 & 6 for the variant.

## Term analysis

### `འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་ (di skad ces)` (discrepancy)

The source text uses 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་' (di skad ces, 'this thus'), which is directly supported by the Sanskrit 'एतद्' (etad, 'this') in 'एतदवोचत्'. However, Commentaries 2 and 6 present a variant reading 'དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་' (de skad ces, 'that thus'). This change from a proximal demonstrative ('this') to a distal demonstrative ('that') constitutes a meaning-altering variant, as it shifts the deictic reference. The source text's reading is confirmed as authoritative by the Sanskrit.
