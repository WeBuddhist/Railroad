## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་ vs དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་**: Commentaries 2 and 6 read 'དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་' (de skad ces, saying that speech) instead of 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་' (di skad ces, saying this speech). The source text and majority of commentaries support 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་', which is adopted as the primary reading.

## Standardized text

No meaning-altering textual variants were found in the source text when compared against the provided commentaries and UCCA semantic interpretation. The minor variation between 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་' and 'དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་' is not considered semantically significant enough to warrant a correction, as both forms serve to introduce the subsequent speech, and the source text's reading is supported by the majority of commentaries and the UCCA.

## Term analysis

### `དེ་ནས་ (de nas)`

The term 'དེ་ནས་' (de nas, then) is consistently used across the source text and all provided commentaries, indicating no discrepancy.

### `སངས་རྒྱས་ཀྱི་མཐུས་ (sangs rgyas kyi mthus)`

The phrase 'སངས་རྒྱས་ཀྱི་མཐུས་' (sangs rgyas kyi mthus, by the power of the Buddha) is consistent across the source text and most commentaries. Commentary 5 uses 'སངས་རྒྱས་ཀྱིས་མཐུས་' (sangs rgyas kyis mthus), which is a minor variation in the ergative particle but does not alter the meaning. Commentary 3 provides an expansion of 'མཐུས་' but preserves the core meaning. No meaning-altering discrepancy found.

### `ཚེ་དང་ལྡན་པ་ཤཱ་རིའི་བུས་ (tshe dang ldan pa shA ri'i bus)`

The phrase 'ཚེ་དང་ལྡན་པ་ཤཱ་རིའི་བུས་' (tshe dang ldan pa shA ri'i bus, the Venerable Śāriputra) is consistent across the source text and all provided commentaries. No discrepancy found.

### `བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོ་འཕགས་པ་སྤྱན་རས་གཟིགས་དབང་ཕྱུག་ལ་ (byang chub sems dpa' chen po 'phags pa spyan ras gzigs dbang phyug la)`

The phrase 'བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོ་འཕགས་པ་སྤྱན་རས་གཟིགས་དབང་ཕྱུག་ལ་' (byang chub sems dpa' chen po 'phags pa spyan ras gzigs dbang phyug la, to the great Bodhisattva, the Noble Avalokiteśvara) is consistent across the source text and most commentaries. Commentary 5 and 8 use 'བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའ་སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོ་འཕགས་པ་', which is a slight repetition of 'སེམས་དཔའ་' but does not alter the meaning. No meaning-altering discrepancy found.

### `འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་ (di skad ces)`

The source text uses 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་' (di skad ces, saying this speech). Commentaries 2 and 6 use 'དེ་སྐད་ཅེས་' (de skad ces, saying that speech). While 'འདི་' (this) and 'དེ་' (that) are demonstrative pronouns with subtle differences in proximity, in this context, both effectively introduce the subsequent direct speech. The majority of commentaries (3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9) support 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་', and the UCCA interpretation also aligns with 'འདི་སྐད་ཅེས་'. This is considered a minor stylistic variant rather than a meaning-altering discrepancy, as the function of introducing the speech remains the same. Therefore, no correction is needed, and the source text's reading is retained.

### `སྨྲས་སོ། (smras so)`

The term 'སྨྲས་སོ།' (smras so, spoke) is consistently used across the source text and all provided commentaries, indicating no discrepancy.
