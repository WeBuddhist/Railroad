## Standardized text

No meaning-altering textual variants were found in the source text. The slight difference in spelling between 'རྣམ་པར་བཞུགས་པའི་' (rnam par bzhugs pa'i) in the source text and 'རྣམ་པར་ཞུགས་པའི་' (rnam par zhugs pa'i) in Commentary 22 is considered a non-meaning-altering variant, likely a scribal alternative for 'abiding' or 'dwelling' in the context of Buddhas across the three times, as supported by the consistent usage of 'བཞུགས་པའི་' in all other commentaries and the UCCA interpretation.

## Term analysis

### `ཕྱིན་ཅི་ལོག་ (phyin ci log)`

The term 'ཕྱིན་ཅི་ལོག་' (misconception/error) is consistently interpreted across commentaries (e.g., Commentary 3, 7, 9, 10, 13, 17) without any meaning-altering variants. The UCCA also aligns with this understanding.

### `མྱ་ངན་ལས་འདས་པར་ (mya ngan las 'das par)`

The phrase 'མྱ་ངན་ལས་འདས་པར་' (to nirvāṇa) is consistently understood as the state of liberation or cessation of suffering across all relevant commentaries (e.g., Commentary 4, 7, 10, 14, 18). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `མཐར་ཕྱིན་ཏོ། (mthar phyin to)`

The verb 'མཐར་ཕྱིན་ཏོ།' (has reached the culmination/end) is consistently interpreted as achieving the ultimate state, particularly nirvāṇa, by the commentaries (e.g., Commentary 4, 10, 18). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `དུས་གསུམ་དུ་ (dus gsum du)`

The phrase 'དུས་གསུམ་དུ་' (in the three times) is consistently explained as referring to the past, present, and future by all relevant commentaries (e.g., Commentary 2, 5, 8, 11). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `རྣམ་པར་བཞུགས་པའི་ (rnam par bzhugs pa'i)`

The term 'རྣམ་པར་བཞུགས་པའི་' (who dwell/are established) is consistently used across most commentaries (e.g., Commentary 2, 5, 8, 11, 15, 19, 23) and the UCCA, conveying the meaning of Buddhas 'abiding' or 'being present' in the three times. Commentary 22 uses 'རྣམ་པར་ཞུགས་པའི་' (rnam par zhugs pa'i). While 'ཞུགས་' typically means 'to enter' or 'to engage', in this context, given the overwhelming consensus for 'བཞུགས་' and the semantic requirement of 'dwelling' for Buddhas across time, 'ཞུགས་' is considered a non-meaning-altering variant, likely a scribal alternative or a less common spelling for the same concept of 'abiding' rather than a distinct semantic shift to 'entered into existence'.

### `སངས་རྒྱས་ཐམས་ཅད་ཀྱང་ (sangs rgyas thams cad kyang)`

The phrase 'སངས་རྒྱས་ཐམས་ཅད་ཀྱང་' (all Buddhas also/even) is consistently understood without any meaning-altering variants across commentaries (e.g., Commentary 5, 8, 11, 15, 19, 22, 23). The UCCA also aligns with this understanding.

### `ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ལ་ (shes rab kyi pha rol tu phyin pa la)`

The phrase 'ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ལ་' (on the Perfection of Wisdom) is consistently interpreted as the object of reliance for enlightenment by all commentaries (e.g., Commentary 5, 8, 11, 15, 19, 21, 22, 23). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `བརྟེན་ནས་ (brten nas)`

The term 'བརྟེན་ནས་' (by relying) is consistently interpreted as the means or basis for achieving enlightenment across all commentaries (e.g., Commentary 2, 5, 8, 11, 15, 19, 21, 22, 23). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `བླ་ན་མེད་པ་ (bla na med pa)`

The term 'བླ་ན་མེད་པ་' (unsurpassable) is consistently defined as 'nothing higher' or 'supreme' by commentaries (e.g., Commentary 5, 6, 8, 22). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `ཡང་དག་པར་ (yang dag par)`

The term 'ཡང་དག་པར་' (truly, perfectly, authentically) is consistently interpreted as 'without error' or 'ultimate reality' by commentaries (e.g., Commentary 6, 8, 22). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `རྫོགས་པའི་ (rdzogs pa'i)`

The term 'རྫོགས་པའི་' (complete) is consistently interpreted as 'perfect' or 'full' by commentaries (e.g., Commentary 6, 22). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `བྱང་ཆུབ་ཏུ་ (byang chub tu)`

The term 'བྱང་ཆུབ་ཏུ་' (to enlightenment) is consistently interpreted as 'realization' or 'dharmadhātu' by commentaries (e.g., Commentary 6, 8, 22). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `མངོན་པར་རྫོགས་པར་ (mngon par rdzogs par)`

The phrase 'མངོན་པར་རྫོགས་པར་' (fully/manifestly complete) is consistently interpreted as 'manifestly awakened' or 'directly perceived' by commentaries (e.g., Commentary 2, 6, 20, 22). No meaning-altering variants were found.

### `སངས་རྒྱས་སོ། (sangs rgyas so)`

The verb 'སངས་རྒྱས་སོ།' (have awakened/become Buddhas) is consistently interpreted as the act of achieving buddhahood across commentaries (e.g., Commentary 8, 20, 22). No meaning-altering variants were found.
