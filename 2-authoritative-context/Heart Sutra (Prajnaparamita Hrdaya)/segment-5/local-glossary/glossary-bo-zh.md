## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **སྙོམས་པར་ཞུགས་སོ། vs སྙོམས་པར་གནས་པ་**: Commentary 5 reads 'སྙོམས་པར་གནས་པ་' (snyoms par gnas pa, 'abided in equipoise'), while the source text and most other commentaries read 'སྙོམས་པར་ཞུགས་སོ།' (snyoms par zhugs so, 'entered into equipoise'). The latter is adopted as the primary reading, consistent with the majority of commentaries and Chinese translations.

## Standardized text

No meaning-altering textual variants were found. The source text, 'དེའི་ཚེ་བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་ཟབ་མོ་སྣང་བ་ཞེས་བྱ་བའི་ཆོས་ཀྱི་རྣམ་གྲངས་ཀྱི་ཏིང་ངེ་འཛིན་ལ་སྙོམས་པར་ཞུགས་སོ། །', is consistent with the authoritative reading as supported by the majority of commentaries and parallel translations. Minor orthographic and grammatical variations in commentaries did not alter the core semantic meaning.

## Term analysis

### `ཏིང་ངེ་འཛིན་ (ting nge 'dzin)`

Commentary 5 uses the abbreviated form 'ཏིང་འཛིན་' (ting 'dzin), and Commentary 7 uses the orthographic variant 'ཏེང་ངེ་འཛིན་' (teng nge 'dzin). These are common contractions or minor orthographic differences and do not alter the meaning of 'samadhi'. The source text's 'ཏིང་ངེ་འཛིན་' is the standard and authoritative form.

### `སྙོམས་པར་ཞུགས་སོ། (snyoms par zhugs so)`

Commentary 5 uses 'སྙོམས་པར་གནས་པ་' (snyoms par gnas pa, 'abided in equipoise') instead of the source text's 'སྙོམས་པར་ཞུགས་སོ།' (snyoms par zhugs so, 'entered into equipoise'). While 'abiding' and 'entering' represent a subtle semantic difference in action versus state, the vast majority of commentaries (1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8) and the provided Chinese translations (using '入' - rù, 'enter') support the source text's reading of 'entered into equipoise'. Therefore, the source text is already the authoritative reading, and no correction is needed. This is noted as a divergence in interpretation rather than a textual correction.

### `དེའི་ཚེ་ (de'i tshe)`

Commentary 7 uses 'དེ་ནས་' (de nas, 'then') in its introductory quote. While 'དེ་ནས་' implies a sequence and 'དེའི་ཚེ་' means 'at that time', they are often used interchangeably in narrative openings. 'དེའི་ཚེ་' is the standard opening for sutras and is supported by all other commentaries and the Chinese translations ('当时' or '尔时'). This is not considered a meaning-altering variant requiring correction.
