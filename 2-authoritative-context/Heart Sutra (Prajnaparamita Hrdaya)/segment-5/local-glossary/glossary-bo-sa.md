## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **ཆོས་ཀྱི་རྣམ་གྲངས་ཀྱི་**: The Tibetan source text includes 'ཆོས་ཀྱི་རྣམ་གྲངས་ཀྱི་' (chos kyi rnam grangs kyi), meaning 'of the category of phenomena', which is not present in the Sanskrit 'गम्भीरावसंबोधं नाम समाधिं' (gambhīrāvasaṃbodhaṃ nāma samādhiṃ). The Sanskrit directly names the samadhi without this additional phrase, and thus the Tibetan has been corrected to align with the Sanskrit.

## Standardized text

དེའི་ཚེ་བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་ཟབ་མོ་སྣང་བ་ཞེས་བྱ་བའི་ཏིང་ངེ་འཛིན་ལ་སྙོམས་པར་ཞུགས་སོ། །

A meaning-altering textual variant was identified where the Tibetan source included 'ཆོས་ཀྱི་རྣམ་གྲངས་ཀྱི་' (chos kyi rnam grangs kyi, 'of the category of phenomena') after the name of the samadhi. This phrase is not present in the authoritative Sanskrit parallel 'गम्भीरावसंबोधं नाम समाधिं' (gambhīrāvasaṃbodhaṃ nāma samādhiṃ), which directly states 'the samadhi named Gambhīrāvasaṃbodha'. The removal of 'ཆོས་ཀྱི་རྣམ་གྲངས་ཀྱི་' standardizes the Tibetan text to align with the Sanskrit, ensuring semantic fidelity. The text is prose, so metrical considerations are not applicable.

## Term analysis

### `ཆོས་ཀྱི་རྣམ་གྲངས་ཀྱི་ (chos kyi rnam grangs kyi)` (discrepancy)

The phrase 'ཆོས་ཀྱི་རྣམ་གྲངས་ཀྱི་' (chos kyi rnam grangs kyi), meaning 'of the category of phenomena', is present in the Tibetan source text but is absent in the authoritative Sanskrit parallel 'गम्भीरावसंबोधं नाम समाधिं' (gambhīrāvasaṃbodhaṃ nāma samādhiṃ). The Sanskrit directly names the samadhi as 'Gambhīrāvasaṃbodha' (Profound Illumination) without an intervening 'category of phenomena'. The Tibetan phrase adds a semantic layer that is not supported by the Sanskrit, thus constituting a meaning-altering variant. The UCCA interpretation of the Tibetan source also reflects this additional layer, structuring 'ཟབ་མོ་སྣང་བ་ཞེས་བྱ་བའི་' as an elaborator of 'ཆོས་ཀྱི་རྣམ་གྲངས་', which then elaborates 'ཏིང་ངེ་འཛིན་'. This differs from the direct naming in Sanskrit.
