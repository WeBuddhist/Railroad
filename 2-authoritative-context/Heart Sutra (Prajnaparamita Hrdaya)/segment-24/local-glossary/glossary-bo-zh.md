## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **སྤྱད་པར་བྱ་ vs བསླབ་པར་བྱ་**: The source text uses 'སྤྱད་པར་བྱ་' (spyad par bya - should be practiced/engaged in). Several commentaries (7, 10, 23, 33) use 'བསླབ་པར་བྱ་' (bslab par bya - should be trained/studied). Both are semantically close in the context of Dharma, but 'སྤྱད་པར་བྱ་' emphasizes active engagement, while 'བསླབ་པར་བྱ་' emphasizes learning. The source reading is retained as primary.

## Standardized text

དེ་ནས་བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་ཏིང་ངེ་འཛིན་དེ་ལས་བཞེངས་ཏེ། བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའ་སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོ་འཕགས་པ་སྤྱན་རས་དབང་ཕྱུག་ལ་ལེགས་སོ་ཞེས་བྱ་བ་བྱིན་ནས། ལེགས་སོ་ལེགས་སོ། རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་དེ་དེ་བཞིན་ནོ། ། རིགས་ཀྱི་བུ་དེ་དེ་བཞིན་ཏེ། ཇི་ལྟར་ཁྱོད་ཀྱིས་བསྟན་པ་བཞིན་དུ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ཟབ་མོ་ལ་སྤྱད་པར་བྱ་སྟེ།དེ་བཞིན་གཤེགས་པ་རྣམས་ཀྱང་རྗེས་སུ་ཡི་རང་ངོ་། །

A meaning-altering grammatical error was corrected. The instrumental case marker 'པོས་' (pos) on 'སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོས་' (sems dpa' chen pos) was changed to the nominative/absolutive 'པོ་' (po) to correctly identify Avalokiteshvara as the recipient of the praise from the Blessed One, rather than the agent giving praise. This correction aligns with the UCCA semantic interpretation and the unanimous interpretation found in all available commentaries. The minor formatting issue of an extra space in 'འཕགས་པ ་' was also corrected to 'འཕགས་པ་'. No metrical changes were required as this was a case marker correction.

## Term analysis

### `སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོས་` (discrepancy)

The instrumental case marker 'པོས་' (pos) on 'སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོས་' (sems dpa' chen pos) incorrectly identifies the Bodhisattva Mahasattva as the agent giving praise. The context, UCCA semantic interpretation, and all available commentaries (e.g., Commentary 1, 9, 12, 18, 26, 32, 34) consistently indicate that the Blessed One gives praise *to* Avalokiteshvara. The correct form should be 'པོ་' (po) (nominative/absolutive) to mark Avalokiteshvara as the recipient of the praise. This is a fundamental change in the grammatical role and meaning of the sentence. Additionally, a minor formatting issue with an extra space in 'འཕགས་པ ་' will be corrected to 'འཕགས་པ་'.

### `སྤྱད་པར་བྱ་སྟེ་`

The term 'སྤྱད་པར་བྱ་སྟེ་' (spyad par bya ste - should be practiced/engaged in) is the reading in the source text. While several commentaries (7, 10, 23, 33) use 'བསླབ་པར་བྱ་' (bslab par bya - should be trained/studied), both terms are semantically plausible in the context of Prajnaparamita. The difference represents a nuance in emphasis (active practice vs. study/training) rather than a fundamental meaning-altering discrepancy. The source text's reading is valid and will be retained, but the alternative reading will be noted as a divergence.
