## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **ཕྱིན་པ་ vs ཕྱིན་མ་**: Commentary 5 uses 'ཕྱིན་མ་ (phyin ma)' instead of 'ཕྱིན་པ་ (phyin pa)'. While 'ཕྱིན་མ་' explicitly feminizes the nominalized verb, 'ཕྱིན་པ་' is the standard form for Prajñāpāramitā, and the feminine aspect is already conveyed by 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་'. This is considered a grammatical nuance rather than a meaning-altering semantic shift.

## Standardized text

No meaning-altering textual variants were found in the source text 'བམ་པོ་གཅིག་གོ །​བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ལ་ཕྱག་འཚལ་ལོ། །'. While Commentary 5 presented 'ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་' (pha rol tu phyin ma) instead of 'ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་' (pha rol du phyin pa), this was determined to be a grammatical nuance emphasizing the feminine aspect, not a fundamental semantic change, as the feminine context is already established by 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་'.

## Term analysis

### `བམ་པོ་གཅིག་གོ`

The phrase 'བམ་པོ་གཅིག་གོ' (bam po gcig go) meaning 'Volume One' is consistent across available translations and does not show any meaning-altering variants in the commentaries. The particle 'གོ' (go) is a common assertive particle.

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ལ་ཕྱག་འཚལ་ལོ།`

The phrase 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ལ་ཕྱག་འཚལ་ལོ།' (bcom ldan 'das ma shes rab kyi pha rol du phyin pa la phyag 'tshal lo) meaning 'Homage to the Bhagavati Prajñāpāramitā' is largely consistent. Commentary 5 uses 'ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་' (pha rol tu phyin ma) instead of 'ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་' (pha rol du phyin pa). The change from 'དུ་' (du) to 'ཏུ་' (tu) is an orthographic variant of the dative particle and does not alter meaning. The change from 'ཕྱིན་པ་' (phyin pa, nominalized verb 'gone/perfected') to 'ཕྱིན་མ་' (phyin ma, 'she who has gone/perfected') adds an explicit feminine suffix. However, 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (Bhagavati) already establishes the feminine context, and 'ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་' is the standard nominalization for Prajñāpāramitā. This is considered a grammatical nuance emphasizing the feminine aspect rather than a fundamental semantic shift, and thus not a meaning-altering discrepancy.
