## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ vs ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་**: The source text uses the general nominalized form 'ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་' (pha rol du phyin pa). Commentary 5 uses 'ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་' (pha rol tu phyin ma), which explicitly marks the feminine gender, aligning with the Sanskrit 'प्रज्ञापारमिता' and the referent 'Bhagavati'. The feminine form is adopted as the primary reading for precision.

## Standardized text

བམ་པོ་གཅིག་གོ །བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་ལ་ཕྱག་འཚལ་ལོ། །

A meaning-refining variant was corrected from 'ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་' (pha rol du phyin pa) to 'ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་' (pha rol tu phyin ma). This change explicitly marks the feminine gender for 'Prajñāpāramitā', aligning with 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (Bhagavati) and the feminine Sanskrit noun 'प्रज्ञापारमिता'. This correction is supported by Commentary 5: 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་ལ་ཕྱག་འཚལ་ལོ།' and the grammatical gender of the Sanskrit equivalent.

## Term analysis

### `བམ་པོ་གཅིག་གོ`

The phrase 'བམ་པོ་གཅིག་གོ' (bam po gcig go) means 'one fascicle'. While some translations interpret this as 'first fascicle', the Tibetan 'གཅིག་' (gcig) unambiguously means 'one', not 'first' ('དང་པོ་'). This is an interpretive nuance in translation, not a meaning-altering variant in the source text itself. No correction is needed for the Tibetan.

### `ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་` (discrepancy)

The source text uses 'ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་' (pha rol du phyin pa), a general nominalized verb form. Commentary 5 uses 'ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་' (pha rol tu phyin ma). The change from 'དུ་' to 'ཏུ་' is a common orthographic variant and not meaning-altering. However, the change from 'ཕྱིན་པ་' to 'ཕྱིན་མ་' is a significant grammatical and semantic refinement. 'ཕྱིན་མ་' explicitly marks the feminine gender, which is appropriate for 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (Bhagavati) and aligns with the Sanskrit 'प्रज्ञापारमिता' (Prajñāpāramitā), a feminine noun. This makes the text more precise and accurate to the intended referent, hence it is considered a meaning-refining variant.
