## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ vs ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་**: Commentary 5 uses 'ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་' (pha rol tu phyin ma), explicitly adding a feminine suffix 'མ་' (ma) and using the 'ཏུ་' (tu) allomorph. The source text uses 'ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་' (pha rol du phyin pa). While grammatically different, the meaning of 'Prajñāpāramitā' as the feminine perfection is maintained in context.

## Term analysis

### `བམ་པོ་གཅིག་གོ`

No meaning-altering variants were found for 'བམ་པོ་གཅིག་གོ' (bam po gcig go). Commentaries and translations consistently interpret this as 'This is the first fascicle'.

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་`

No meaning-altering variants were found for 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (bcom ldan 'das ma). This term consistently refers to the Bhagavati.

### `ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་`

The phrase 'ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་དུ་ཕྱིན་པ་' (shes rab kyi pha rol du phyin pa) refers to Prajñāpāramitā, the Perfection of Wisdom. Commentary 5 presents 'ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་' (pha rol tu phyin ma). The change from 'དུ་' (du) to 'ཏུ་' (tu) is a common orthographic variation of the allomorphs for the locative/directional case marker and does not alter meaning. The change from 'ཕྱིན་པ་' (phyin pa) to 'ཕྱིན་མ་' (phyin ma) explicitly adds a feminine suffix. However, given that 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (Bhagavati) already establishes the feminine context, 'ཕྱིན་པ་' is conventionally understood as referring to the feminine perfection. Therefore, this grammatical clarification is not considered a meaning-altering variant.

### `ཕྱག་འཚལ་ལོ`
