## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **ཕྱིན་པ་ vs ཕྱིན་མ་**: The source text and Commentaries 2 and 8 use `ཕྱིན་པ་` (phyin pa), referring to the abstract perfection. Commentary 5 uses `ཕྱིན་མ་` (phyin ma), referring to the feminine deity Prajñāpāramitā, which is adopted as the primary reading for consistency with `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་`.

## Standardized text

བམ་པོ་གཅིག་གོ །བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་ལ་ཕྱག་འཚལ་ལོ། །

The term `ཕྱིན་པ་` (phyin pa) was corrected to `ཕྱིན་མ་` (phyin ma) to align with the feminine honorific `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་` (bcom ldan 'das ma, Bhagavati), thereby clarifying the referent as the feminine deity Prajñāpāramitā rather than the abstract perfection. This correction is supported by Commentary 5, which explicitly uses `ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་མ་`. Additionally, the dative particle `དུ་` (du) was standardized to `ཏུ་` (tu) in `ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་` for grammatical consistency with the preceding `ལ་` (la) in `ཕ་རོལ་` (pha rol), although this is an orthographic rather than a meaning-altering change.

## Term analysis

### `ཕྱིན་པ་ (phyin pa)` (discrepancy)

The source text uses `ཕྱིན་པ་` (phyin pa), which can refer to the abstract perfection of wisdom. However, given the preceding `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་` (bcom ldan 'das ma, Bhagavati), a feminine honorific, the reading `ཕྱིན་མ་` (phyin ma) found in Commentary 5 is more grammatically consistent and semantically precise. It explicitly refers to the feminine deity Prajñāpāramitā (the Mother), rather than the abstract concept. This constitutes a meaning-altering change, clarifying the specific referent of the homage.
