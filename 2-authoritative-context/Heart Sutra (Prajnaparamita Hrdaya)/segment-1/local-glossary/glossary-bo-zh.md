## Term analysis

### `བྷ་ག་བ་ཏི་པྲ་ཛྙ་པ་ར་མི་ཏཱྀ་ཧྲད་ཡ།` (discrepancy)

The Sanskrit transliteration in the source text contains multiple meaning-altering errors compared to the standard 'Bhagavatī Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya'. Specifically: 1) 'བ་' (ba) should be 'ཝ་' (wa) for 'va'. 2) 'པྲ་ཛྙ་' (pra dznya) lacks the long 'ā' marker, which should be 'པྲ་ཛྙཱ་' (pra dznyA) for 'Prajñā'. 3) 'པ་ར་མི་ཏཱྀ་' (pa ra mi tI) is an incorrect transliteration, likely a typo, and should be 'པཱ་ར་མི་ཏཱ་' (pA ra mi tA) for 'Pāramitā'. 4) 'ཧྲད་ཡ' (hrad ya) is missing the syllable 'ད་' (da) and incorrectly transliterates 'ṛ' as 'ra', it should be 'ཧྲི་ད་ཡ་' (hri da ya) for 'Hṛdaya'. These corrections are essential for the accurate representation of the Sanskrit title and are supported by standard Sanskrit orthography and commentary references (e.g., Commentary 7: 'བྷ་ག་ཝ་ཏི་པྲ་ཛྙཱ་པཱ་ར་མི་ཏ་ཧྲི་ད་ཡ་ཞེས་བྱུང་།').
