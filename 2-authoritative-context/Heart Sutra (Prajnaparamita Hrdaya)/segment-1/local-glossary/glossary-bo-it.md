## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **པྲ་ཛྙ་ vs པྲ་ཛྙཱ་**: The source text 'པྲ་ཛྙ་' lacks the long 'ā' vowel mark. The corrected 'པྲ་ཛྙཱ་' aligns with standard Sanskrit 'Prajñā' and Commentary 7.
⚑ **པ་ར་མི་ཏཱྀ་ vs པཱ་ར་མི་ཏཱ་**: The source text 'པ་ར་མི་ཏཱྀ་' uses an incorrect final vowel 'īṃ'. The corrected 'པཱ་ར་མི་ཏཱ་' aligns with standard Sanskrit 'Pāramitā'.
⚑ **ཧྲད་ཡ vs ཧྲི་ད་ཡ་**: The source text 'ཧྲད་ཡ' is an inaccurate transliteration. The corrected 'ཧྲི་ད་ཡ་' aligns with standard Sanskrit 'Hṛdaya' and Commentary 7.

## Standardized text

༄༅། །རྒྱ་གར་སྐད་དུ། བྷ་ག་བ་ཏི་པྲ་ཛྙཱ་པཱ་ར་མི་ཏཱ་ཧྲི་ད་ཡ།

Meaning-altering transliteration inaccuracies were corrected based on the standard Sanskrit title 'भगवती प्रज्ञापारमिता हृदय' (Bhagavatī Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya). Specifically, 'པྲ་ཛྙ་' was corrected to 'པྲ་ཛྙཱ་' to include the long 'ā' vowel, 'པ་ར་མི་ཏཱྀ་' was corrected to 'པཱ་ར་མི་ཏཱ་' to reflect the correct final 'ā' vowel of 'Pāramitā', and 'ཧྲད་ཡ' was corrected to 'ཧྲི་ད་ཡ་' for an accurate representation of 'Hṛdaya'. These corrections ensure the Tibetan transliteration precisely matches the authoritative Sanskrit source.

## Term analysis

### `རྒྱ་གར་ (rgya gar)`

No discrepancy. The term 'རྒྱ་གར་' (India) is consistent across sources and accurately transliterates the Sanskrit context.

### `སྐད་དུ། (skad du)`

No discrepancy. The term 'སྐད་དུ།' (in the language) is consistent across sources and accurately reflects the meaning.

### `བྷ་ག་བ་ཏི་ (bha ga ba ti)`

The source's transliteration 'བྷ་ག་བ་ཏི་' (Bhagavatī) is a reasonable representation of the Sanskrit 'भगवती'. Commentary 7 uses 'བྷ་ག་ཝ་ཏི་', which is also acceptable. No meaning-altering discrepancy.

### `པྲ་ཛྙ་ (pra jnya)` (discrepancy)

The source text 'པྲ་ཛྙ་' (Prajña) lacks the long 'ā' vowel mark present in the standard Sanskrit 'प्रज्ञा' (Prajñā) and in Commentary 7's 'པྲ་ཛྙཱ་'. This is a transliteration inaccuracy that should be corrected to accurately reflect the Sanskrit pronunciation, thus altering the precise representation of the term.

### `པ་ར་མི་ཏཱྀ་ (pa ra mi tīṃ)` (discrepancy)

The source text 'པ་ར་མི་ཏཱྀ་' (Pāramitīṃ) is an inaccurate transliteration of the Sanskrit 'पारमिता' (Pāramitā). The final vowel 'īṃ' (ཏཱྀ་) should be 'ā' (ཏཱ་). This is a significant phonetic and orthographic deviation from the authoritative Sanskrit, warranting correction to 'པཱ་ར་མི་ཏཱ་' to accurately represent the term.

### `ཧྲད་ཡ། (hrad ya)` (discrepancy)

The source text 'ཧྲད་ཡ' (Hradaya) is a significantly inaccurate transliteration of the Sanskrit 'हृदय' (Hṛdaya). Commentary 7 provides the correct transliteration 'ཧྲི་ད་ཡ་'. This phonetic and orthographic deviation alters the correct representation of the Sanskrit term and requires correction.
