## Standardized text

༄༅། །རྒྱ་གར་སྐད་དུ། བྷ་ག་ཝ་ཏི་པྲ་ཛྙཱ་པཱ་ར་མི་ཏ་ཧྲི་ད་ཡ།

The Sanskrit title has been corrected to `Bhagavatī Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya` based on standard Sanskrit transliteration practices and explicit confirmation from Commentary 7, which states: 'སྙིང་པོ་འདིའི་མཚན་ལ། བྷ་ག་ཝ་ཏི་པྲ་ཛྙཱ་པཱ་ར་མི་ཏ་ཧྲི་ད་ཡ་ཞེས་བྱུང་།' (The name of this Heart [Sutra] is Bhagavatī Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya). Specific corrections include: `བ་` (ba) to `ཝ་` (wa) in `Bhagavatī`; `ཛྙ་` (dznya) to `ཛྙཱ་` (dznyā), `པ་ར་` (pa ra) to `པཱ་ར་` (pā ra), and `མི་ཏཱྀ་` (mi tī) to `མི་ཏ་` (mi ta) in `Prajñāpāramitā`; and `ཧྲད་ཡ་` (hrad ya) to `ཧྲི་ད་ཡ་` (hri da ya) in `Hṛdaya`.

## Term analysis

### `བྷ་ག་བ་ཏི་ (bhaga ba ti)` (discrepancy)

The source text uses `བ་` (ba) for 'va' in `Bhagavatī`. The more accurate and standard transliteration, as explicitly stated in Commentary 7 (`བྷ་ག་ཝ་ཏི་`), uses `ཝ་` (wa). This is a meaning-altering correction of the Sanskrit transliteration.

### `པྲ་ཛྙ་པ་ར་མི་ཏཱྀ་ (pra dznya pa ra mi tī)` (discrepancy)

This term for `Prajñāpāramitā` contains multiple transliteration errors in the source text. `ཛྙ་` (dznya) should be `ཛྙཱ་` (dznyā) (missing long 'ā'). `པ་ར་` (pa ra) should be `པཱ་ར་` (pā ra) (missing long 'ā'). `མི་ཏཱྀ་` (mi tī) should be `མི་ཏ་` (mi ta) (incorrect vowel for 'tā'). Commentary 7 confirms the correct form `པྲ་ཛྙཱ་པཱ་ར་མི་ཏ་`. These corrections are meaning-altering as they rectify the Sanskrit word itself.

### `ཧྲད་ཡ་ (hrad ya)` (discrepancy)

The source text uses `ཧྲད་ཡ་` (hrad ya) for `Hṛdaya`. The standard and correct transliteration, confirmed by Commentary 7, is `ཧྲི་ད་ཡ་` (hri da ya). This is a clear meaning-altering correction of the Sanskrit word.
