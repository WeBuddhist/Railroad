## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པའི་སྔགས vs མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པར་བྱེད་པའི་སྔགས**: Commentary 34 (Ul8k7VmgZPfGXKUBTHAu2) reads 'མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པར་བྱེད་པའི་སྔགས' (mi mnyam pa dang mnyam par byed pa'i sngags), which implies 'the mantra that *makes* equal to the unequalled'. The primary reading 'མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པའི་སྔགས' (mi mnyam pa dang mnyam pa'i sngags) is supported by the UCCA and other commentaries (C6, C15, C22, C29) and means 'the mantra that *is* equal to the unequalled'. The primary reading is retained as authoritative.

## Standardized text

Therefore, no standardization was required.

### `བླ་ན་མེད་པའི་སྔགས (bla na med pa'i sngags)`

The phrase 'བླ་ན་མེད་པའི་སྔགས' (bla na med pa'i sngags, unsurpassed mantra) is consistent across the source text and all available commentaries. No meaning-altering variant found.

### `མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པའི་སྔགས (mi mnyam pa dang mnyam pa'i sngags)`

Commentary 34 reads 'མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པར་བྱེད་པའི་སྔགས' (mi mnyam pa dang mnyam par byed pa'i sngags), adding 'བྱེད་པའི་' (byed pa'i - 'that makes'). This would semantically shift the meaning from 'the mantra that *is* equal to the unequalled' to 'the mantra that *makes* equal to the unequalled'. However, the source text's reading is supported by the UCCA interpretation and other commentaries (C6, C15, C22, C29) which interpret 'མཉམ་པའི་' as 'equal to' without the causative 'བྱེད་པའི་'. Therefore, the source text is considered authoritative, and no correction is applied. This is noted as a divergence.
