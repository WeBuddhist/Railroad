## Gloss Divergences

⚑ **མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པའི་སྔགས vs མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པར་བྱེད་པའི་སྔགས**: The source text and most commentaries (C6, C10, C15, C22, C29, C33) read 'mantra that *is* equal to the unequalled' (descriptive). Commentary 34, however, reads 'mantra that *makes* equal to the unequalled' (causative). The descriptive reading is adopted as primary.

## Standardized text

No meaning-altering textual variants were found that required standardization of the source text. One minor semantic difference was observed in Commentary 34 regarding the phrase 'མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པའི་སྔགས' (mi mnyam pa dang mnyam pa'i sngags), where it used a causative form ('makes equal') instead of the descriptive form ('is equal') found in the source and supported by the majority of other commentaries. This difference is noted in the 'divergences' section but does not warrant a correction of the authoritative source text.

## Term analysis

### `དེ་ལྟ་བས་ན་ (de lta bas na)`

This introductory phrase 'therefore' is consistently used across all relevant commentaries and the UCCA interpretation, indicating no meaning-altering variant.

### `ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྔགས (shes rab kyi pha rol tu phyin pa'i sngags)`

The phrase 'mantra of the Perfection of Wisdom' appears multiple times and is consistently rendered and interpreted across all commentaries and the UCCA, with no meaning-altering variants.

### `རིག་པ་ཆེན་པོའི་སྔགས (rig pa chen po'i sngags)`

The phrase 'mantra of great awareness/knowledge' is consistently used and interpreted across all commentaries and the UCCA, with no meaning-altering variants.

### `བླ་ན་མེད་པའི་སྔགས (bla na med pa'i sngags)`

The phrase 'unsurpassable mantra' is consistently used and interpreted across all commentaries and the UCCA, with no meaning-altering variants.

### `མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པའི་སྔགས (mi mnyam pa dang mnyam pa'i sngags)`

The source text reads 'mantra that is equal to the unequalled' (descriptive). Commentary 34 presents a variant 'མི་མཉམ་པ་དང་མཉམ་པར་བྱེད་པའི་སྔགས' (mi mnyam pa dang mnyam par byed pa'i sngags), which means 'mantra that *makes* equal to the unequalled' (causative). While this is a semantic difference, the majority of commentaries (C6, C10, C15, C22, C29, C33) and the UCCA interpretation support the descriptive reading of the source text. Therefore, the source text is considered authoritative, and this variant, though noted, does not fundamentally alter the meaning in a way that necessitates correction of the source text.

### `སྡུག་བསྔལ་ཐམས་ཅད་རབ་ཏུ་ཞི་བར་བྱེད་པའི་སྔགས (sdug bsngal thams cad rab tu zhi bar byed pa'i sngags)`

The phrase 'mantra that completely pacifies all suffering' is consistently used and interpreted across all commentaries and the UCCA, with no meaning-altering variants.

### `མི་རྫུན་པས་ན་བདེན་པར་ཤེས་པར་བྱ་སྟེ (mi rdzun pas na bden par shes par bya ste)`

The phrase 'because it is not false, it should be known as true' is consistently used and interpreted across all commentaries and the UCCA, with no meaning-altering variants.

### `སྨྲས་པ (smras pa)`

The verb 'was spoken' (perfective) is consistently used and interpreted across all commentaries and the UCCA, with no meaning-altering variants.
