## Standardized text

The source text, 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྙིང་པོ།', represents the full and authoritative title of the Prajnaparamita Heart Sutra. ## Term analysis

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་`

The term 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (bcom ldan 'das ma, Bhagavati) is present in the source text and fully supported by Commentary 4, which provides the complete title. While other commentaries (1, 2, 3, 5, 6) omit this term in their own titles, these are considered common abbreviations or stylistic choices for the commentary's title, not a meaning-altering textual variant of the primary source text itself. The source text's inclusion of 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' is authoritative.

### `ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་`

The phrase 'ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་' (shes rab kyi pha rol tu phyin pa'i, of the Perfection of Wisdom) is present in the source text and fully supported by Commentary 4. Commentaries 2 and 3 use the abbreviation 'ཤེར་ཕྱིན་' (sher phyin), and Commentaries 5 and 6 omit the 'ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་' part entirely, using 'ཤེས་རབ་སྙིང་པོ་' (shes rab snying po). These are common and acceptable abbreviations or stylistic choices for commentary titles, not meaning-altering textual variants of the primary source text. The source text's full phrase is authoritative.

### `སྙིང་པོ།`

The term 'སྙིང་པོ།' (snying po, Heart/Essence) is consistently present across the source text and all relevant commentaries (when referring to the sutra title). No meaning-altering variants were found for this term.
