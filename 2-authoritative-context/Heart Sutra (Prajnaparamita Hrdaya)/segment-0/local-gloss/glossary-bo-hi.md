No meaning-altering discrepancies were found in the source text. The Tibetan title 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྙིང་པོ།' perfectly aligns with the Sanskrit 'भगवती प्रज्ञापारमिता हृदय', confirming its authoritative reading. Other commentaries use common abbreviations which do not alter the meaning.

## Term analysis

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་`

The term 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (bcom ldan 'das ma), translating to 'Bhagavatī', in the source text is directly supported by the Sanskrit 'भगवती' (Bhagavatī) found in the parallel translations. While Commentary 1 uses 'འཕགས་པ་' (phags pa, Ārya), this represents a different term for the title's opening, but it does not indicate a meaning-altering error in the source text, which is already aligned with the highest authority (Sanskrit). Therefore, no correction to the source text is required.

### `ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་`

The phrase 'ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་' (shes rab kyi pha rol tu phyin pa'i), translating to 'of the Perfection of Wisdom' or 'Prajñāpāramitā's', is consistent across the source text, Commentary 4, and the Sanskrit 'प्रज्ञापारमिता' (Prajñāpāramitā). Commentaries 2, 3, 5, and 6 use abbreviated forms like 'ཤེར་ཕྱིན་' or 'ཤེར་སྙིང་', which are common stylistic contractions and not meaning-altering variants of the full phrase. No discrepancy found.

### `སྙིང་པོ།`

The term 'སྙིང་པོ་' (snying po), translating to 'Heart' or 'Essence', is consistent across the source text, Commentary 4, and the Sanskrit 'हृदय' (Hṛdaya). No meaning-altering discrepancy found.
