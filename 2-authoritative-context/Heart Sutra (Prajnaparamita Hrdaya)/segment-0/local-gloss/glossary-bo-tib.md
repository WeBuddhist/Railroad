## Standardized text

The source text `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྙིང་པོ།` is the standard and authoritative title of the Prajñāpāramitāhṛdaya Sūtra. While Commentary 1 presents a variant 'འཕགས་པ་' (Noble One) instead of 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (Bhagavati), which is a semantic difference, Commentary 4 fully corroborates the source text's reading. Given the authoritative nature of the title and the lack of consensus among commentaries for a correction, no standardization is applied to the source text.

## Term analysis

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་` (discrepancy)

Commentary 1 presents 'འཕགས་པ་' (phags pa, Noble One/Ārya) as a variant for 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (bcom ldan 'das ma, Bhagavati). This is a semantic substitution, as 'འཕགས་པ་' is a general term for a noble being, while 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' specifically refers to a female Buddha or goddess, often identified with Prajñāpāramitā herself. This constitutes a meaning-altering variant. However, the source text is the established title of the sutra, and Commentary 4 matches it exactly, indicating the source reading is authoritative. Therefore, no correction is applied to the source text.
