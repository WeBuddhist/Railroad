## Gloss Divergences

- ⚑ **བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ vs འཕགས་པ་**: Commentary 1's title uses 'འཕགས་པ་' (phags pa, Noble) instead of 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (bcom ldan 'das ma, Blessed Mother). While both are honorifics, they are distinct terms. The source text's reading of 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' is supported by Commentary 4 and several translations, and is adopted as primary.
- ⚑ **Full title vs. Abbreviated forms**: Commentaries 2, 3, 5, and 6 use abbreviated forms such as 'ཤེར་ཕྱིན་སྙིང་པོའི་' or 'ཤེར་སྙིང་' which omit 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' and shorten 'ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་'. These are stylistic abbreviations rather than semantic variants of the full title.

No meaning-altering textual variants were found in the source text that would require correction. The reading 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྙིང་པོ།' is well-supported by Commentary 4 and multiple traditional translations, confirming its authoritative status. While Commentary 1 presents an alternative honorific ('འཕགས་པ་') and other commentaries use abbreviated forms, these do not indicate an error in the source text's full title.

## Term analysis

### `བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་`

The term 'བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་མ་' (bcom ldan 'das ma, Blessed Mother) in the source text is a valid and widely attested honorific for the Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya Sūtra. While Commentary 1 uses 'འཕགས་པ་' (phags pa, Noble) as an alternative honorific, this does not indicate an error in the source text itself. Commentary 4 and several translations (e.g., Chinese '世尊母' and '具德佛母') support the source text's reading. Therefore, no meaning-altering discrepancy requiring correction of the source text is found.

### `ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྙིང་པོ།`

The phrase 'ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྙིང་པོ།' (shes rab kyi pha rol tu phyin pa'i snying po, Heart/Essence of the Perfection of Wisdom) is consistent across relevant commentaries and translations, with some commentaries using abbreviated forms for brevity, which are not considered meaning-altering variants.
